1 Mission & the two-site model

NWCA runs two websites, and Google knows they are the same business — both carry sameAs schema pointing at each other and share the same name, address, and phone (entity stitching, live on both sides since July 12, 2026). That is deliberate: it pools brand authority into one knowledge-graph entity.

The one consequence that shapes everything: if both sites publish pages chasing the same search query, Google splits ranking strength between them and often ranks neither on page 1. We watched this happen on .net itself — “custom embroidery near me” split across two of its own pages held both on page 2. Across two domains it is worse. So the rule is not “compete gently”; it is never compete at all: every query class has exactly one owner, and the other site links to it.

The strategy in one sentence: .net wins the customer’s trust and location (“who does embroidery near me?”) — teamnwca wins the customer’s product decision and order (“which garment, what price?”) — and each site hands the visitor to the other at exactly the moment their question changes.

Division of labor: Mehar (umehar56@gmail.com) runs .net per the Recovery Roadmap. Claude runs teamnwca per this page (weekly blog autopilot + product/brand SEO). Erik owns gray-area calls and relays questions between the two. The full doctrine was decided July 13–14, 2026 (“Lane Split v2”) and sent to Mehar as a 4-document packet on July 14.

The 30-second test for any new content idea

  1. Is the searcher asking WHERE to get it done, or WHO to trust locally? → .net city/service page.
  2. Is the searcher asking HOW something works? → .net educational post.
  3. Is the searcher deciding WHICH product/brand to buy, or asking about PRICE? → teamnwca post, brand page, or tool.
  4. Could it honestly go either way? → flag to Erik before writing. Two minutes now beats six months of split rankings.
Hi-vis / safety, said once: .net owns the educational angle (ANSI class explainers, “WA hi-vis requirements” — excellent post, link it). teamnwca owns the commercial terms (product picks, “hi-vis shirts with company logo”, the safety-stripes program, /custom-safety-apparel). The ~50 near-duplicate hi-vis drafts in .net’s backup get merged into its educational winners — never published standalone.

Hard rules (never break)

  • Rule 9 — one pricing engine. No price appears outside it, on either domain. No prices/offers in any JSON-LD schema.
  • Never chase “near me” or city queries — that lane is .net’s recovery plan.
  • Never write educational/craft content — link .net’s explainer instead.
  • Never copy .net text (literal duplicate content tanks both). Check reference/nwcustomapparel-net-blogs/INDEX.md before writing.
  • Slugs never change after publish. One post/week — steady cadence beats dumps.
  • Every post: 3+ product links, one tool CTA, one .net cross-link where a related explainer exists, meta ≤155 chars written for the click.

4 nwcustomapparel.net — Mehar’s recovery plan

Summary of the SEMrush/Claude roadmap delivered July 14, 2026 (full doc in the reference shelf). Mehar executes; this is here so anyone can see what the other half of the strategy is doing.

The diagnosis

  • Ranking keywords peaked ~15–18K in early 2025, now ~4–5K; ~1,000 visits/mo and falling ~12–16%.
  • ~47% of traffic is the homepage on brand searches; ~35% is informational posts about other brands (Carhartt, Eddie Bauer) — traffic that Google’s helpful-content updates + AI Overviews are structurally eroding and that rarely converts.
  • The commercial/local “money pages” already exist but sit on pages 2–5 — e.g. /custom-embroidery-seattle/ holds 206 keywords yet earns 5 visits/mo.
  • Proof the fix works: the new Bonney Lake local page already pulls 32 visits/mo at ~#10 for “custom embroidery near me” (8,100 searches/mo).

His four phases

PhaseFocus
1 · Weeks 1–3Fix “near me” cannibalization (one page per city per term) · on-page overhaul of the money pages · add CTAs from top informational posts to commercial pages · refresh the biggest decliners.
2 · Weeks 3–8Local landing-page system to the Bonney Lake standard — rebuild Seattle/Spokane/Portland/Kent, add Tacoma, Milton, Puyallup, Federal Way, Auburn… URL pattern /custom-embroidery-{city}-wa/, 301 old variants · optimize Google Business Profile + citations + reviews.
3 · Weeks 6–14Content triage of ~300 posts (keep/merge/retire; hi-vis drafts merged, not published) · pivot new content to commercial/local/industry topics · keep AI-citation-friendly structure (497 citations today).
4 · OngoingGrow referring domains (615 → competitor benchmark ~1,300) · close keyword gaps vs brokenarrowwear / thefoundryclothing.
Anti-doorway rule (his playbook, worth repeating): city pages are NOT find-and-replace clones — each needs ~40–50% genuinely unique local content (real neighborhoods, industries, drive times, local reviews) or Google suppresses the whole set. The Bonney Lake page ranks because it’s genuinely useful.

7 Scoreboard & checkpoints

Review monthly (with Mehar’s report)

  • Non-brand organic clicks, both properties (Search Console) — the real growth number; brand searches don’t count.
  • Quote requests / form fills from organic, whichever site they land on — it’s one pipeline (watch Forms Inbox referrers).
  • SEMrush keyword counts both domains — teamnwca baseline 42 (Jul 12, 2026); .net decline vs its −9%/mo baseline.
  • Cross-domain click-through — .net → teamnwca catalog clicks and the reverse.
  • Zero-cannibalization check — no query where both domains sit on pages 1–3 with the same intent; if found, pick one owner and de-optimize/301 the other.

October 2026 checkpoint

Three months of curves on both sites, reviewed together. Success looks like:

  • teamnwca: 12+ posts live and indexed; style-number queries ranking (those move fastest); keyword count climbing steadily from 42.
  • .net: money pages moving page 2 → page 1 on the target commercial terms; local pack visibility for “near me” searches; informational bleed slowed.
  • Business: quote requests arriving with blog/product referrers.
  • Then: revisit the long-term one-domain-vs-two question with data (deliberately deferred — no merge for now, Erik’s call July 12).
