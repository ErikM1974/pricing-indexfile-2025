How to read this document

This isn't a teardown — the site is genuinely well-built, and the fundamentals (clean technical setup, deep content, solid on-page optimization) are done right. The problem is strategic, not technical: the site's traffic was built on informational articles that Google and AI have been devaluing since early 2025, while the pages that actually win customers — local, commercial "custom embroidery / printing" pages — are built but not ranking. This plan redirects the existing engine toward the traffic that converts. Sections in order: where we stand, what happened and why, the core opportunity, and a phased 90-day+ roadmap with specific pages and keywords.

1. Executive summary

Organic visibility peaked in early–mid 2025 and has since fallen by roughly two-thirds. The decline is concentrated in informational blog content; the commercial pages that should drive revenue are underperforming. The fix is a deliberate shift from "traffic for traffic's sake" to local + commercial intent.

  • The trend: ranking keywords peaked near 15,000–18,000 in early 2025 and are now ~4,000–5,000 — a sustained multi-quarter decline, not a blip. Monthly organic traffic is ~1,000 visits (US), down ~12–16% recently.
  • The cause: ~35% of traffic comes from informational articles about other brands (Carhartt, Eddie Bauer) and generic topics — exactly what Google's 2024–2025 "helpful content" / core updates and AI Overviews have hit hardest. Another ~47% is the homepage on your own brand name.
  • The missed opportunity: your commercial and local pages already target the right keywords but rank on page 2–5. Example: /custom-embroidery-seattle/ holds 206 keywords yet earns just 5 visits/month. The high-value term "custom embroidery near me" (8,100 searches/mo) sits at position ~10–35.
  • Proof the fix works: a new local page, /embroidery-screenprinting-bonneylake-wa/, is already pulling 32 visits/month for "custom embroidery near me" — the model to replicate.
  • Realistic goal (6–9 months): rebuild qualified traffic — local, commercial, lead-generating — even if raw informational traffic keeps sliding. Chasing back the informational peak is neither likely nor valuable.

Root causes (in order of impact):

  • Google's 2024–2025 Helpful Content & core updates. These systematically demoted broad informational content from sites without deep topical authority on that subject — e.g., an apparel decorator ranking for "who invented the hoodie." Much of this is industry-wide, not a mistake anyone made.
  • AI Overviews are eating informational clicks. Questions like "how to soften a Carhartt jacket" increasingly get answered directly in Google/AI, so even a #1 ranking now earns fewer clicks. Your visibility here is fine; the clicks are structurally shrinking.
  • Over-reliance on tangential topics. The content engine chased what was easy to rank for (low-difficulty informational terms about other brands) rather than what's hard but valuable (competitive local commercial terms).

4. The recovery roadmap

Sequenced so the fastest, highest-certainty wins come first. Each item names the page(s), the action, and why it matters. Rough timeline assumes one focused person; adjust to Mehar's bandwidth.

Phase 1 · Weeks 1–3 Quick wins & stop the bleeding

  • CriticalFix the "near me" cannibalization. Decide one primary page per city for "custom embroidery near me" / "embroidery services near me." Point the others' internal links and any duplicate targeting at the primary; differentiate the secondary pages' focus (e.g., Seattle page → "custom embroidery Seattle," Bonney Lake → "near me"). Splitting the 8,100/mo term across two page-2 URLs is costing you page-1 potential on both.
  • CriticalOn-page overhaul of the top money pages (/custom-embroidery-seattle/, /seattle-custom-t-shirts/, /direct-to-garment/, /custom-printed-t-shirts.../): tighten title + H1 to the exact commercial target, add location + intent modifiers, strengthen the intro, add pricing/process/CTA blocks, and internal-link them from the homepage and top nav. They already have keyword coverage; they need on-page focus and internal authority.
  • HighFunnel your informational traffic to commercial pages. The Carhartt/Eddie Bauer/cotton-polyester posts pull ~35% of traffic — add prominent, relevant internal links and CTAs from them to the matching service pages (e.g., Carhartt jacket post → custom embroidered Carhartt jackets service). Capture the visitors you already have.
  • HighRefresh the biggest losers that are still on-topic and commercial-adjacent (/carhartt-alternatives-bulk-workwear/, /why-is-carhartt-so-popular/): update for 2026, add a clear commercial angle/CTA. For purely informational decliners with no commercial tie, don't over-invest.
  • VerifyConfirm LocalBusiness schema & the www→non-www 301 (flagged in the technical audit) are correctly in place — quick items that support everything local.

Phase 2 · Weeks 3–8 Win local & commercial intent

  • CriticalBuild out a proper local landing-page system, modeled on the winning Bonney Lake page, for every service area you serve: Tacoma, Milton, Seattle, Kent, Puyallup, Federal Way, Auburn, etc. Each page: unique local copy, embedded map/NAP, local reviews, service list, and internal links. (You already have Seattle/Spokane/Portland/Kent stubs ranking at 0 — rebuild these to the Bonney Lake standard rather than starting over.)
  • CriticalOptimize Google Business Profile + local citations. Local pack rankings drive the "near me" searches as much as the website. Ensure consistent NAP across the web, categories, photos, services, and a steady flow of reviews (you have a reviews plugin already).
  • HighRescue the service/category pages (/services/, /services/custom-t-shirt-printing/, /services/custom-patch/, /direct-to-garment/): these are the commercial backbone and currently earn ~0. Expand content depth, add proof (case studies, brands you decorate — Carhartt, Nike, North Face), and link them prominently.
  • HighTarget the high-volume commercial terms deliberately: "custom embroidery near me" (8.1K), "embroidery services near me" (2.4K), "custom t-shirt printing [city]," "screen printing near me," "DTF printing services near me." Build/assign one strong page each and track weekly.

Phase 3 · Weeks 6–14 Pivot the content strategy

  • HighTriage all ~300 posts into keep / merge / repurpose / retire. Keep & monetize the informational winners (add CTAs/links). Merge thin near-duplicates (the backup shows ~50 near-identical "high-vis" and "baseball cap" drafts — do not publish these as-is; they're thin duplicate content risk). Retire or noindex dead weight that earns nothing and has no commercial purpose.
  • HighShift new content toward bottom-of-funnel & commercial comparison topics that fit your business and convert: "custom embroidery cost calculator," "screen printing vs embroidery for uniforms," "bulk polo pricing," industry-specific pages (HVAC, construction, restaurants — the HVAC page is already showing signs of life). Less "who invented the hoodie," more "custom embroidered work shirts for [industry] in Washington."
  • MediumLean into AI search. You're already cited 497×. Keep content well-structured (clear headings, direct answers, FAQ schema, factual specifics) so you keep earning AI Overview / ChatGPT citations as that channel grows.

Phase 4 · Ongoing Authority & competitive ground

  • MediumGrow referring domains. You have 615; the market leader below has ~1,300. Pursue local partnerships, supplier/brand pages (SanMar, Carhartt authorized decorator), chamber/industry directories, PR on the "since 1977 / oldest in the Northwest" story, and customer/case-study links.
  • MediumStudy the winners' commercial pages (see competitor table) and close the keyword gap on terms they rank for and you don't.

6. How to measure recovery

Track outcomes, not vanity. Review monthly:

  • Non-brand organic traffic (exclude "nw custom apparel" etc.) — the real growth metric. Total traffic will be noisy while informational content keeps sliding; non-brand commercial traffic is what matters.
  • Rankings for the target commercial terms — set up SEMrush Position Tracking for "custom embroidery near me," "embroidery services near me," "custom t-shirt printing [city]," etc. Watch page-2→page-1 movement.
  • Traffic to service & local pages specifically — the money pages in section 3c should climb from ~0.
  • Leads / form fills / quote requests from organic — connect Search Console + analytics to actual inquiries. This is the number the business cares about.
  • Local pack / Google Business Profile visibility for "near me" searches.
Set expectations: SEO recovery runs on a 3–6 month clock, and local/commercial rankings take time to earn. Expect early movement on the quick wins (cannibalization fix, on-page, internal linking) within 4–8 weeks, and the local build-out to compound over a quarter or two. The goal is qualified traffic that converts — don't judge success by chasing the 2025 informational peak back.
