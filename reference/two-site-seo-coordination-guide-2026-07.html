How nwcustomapparel.net and teamnwca.com work together

One business, two websites, zero overlap — who writes what, who links where, and why it makes both sites rank better. For Mehar.

Prepared for: Mehar (web/SEO) · Commissioned by: Erik Mickelson · July 14, 2026 · Companion files: SEO Audit & Traffic Recovery Roadmap, Local Landing Page Playbook, Local Landing Page Template

Why this document exists

Northwest Custom Apparel runs two websites, and Google knows they're the same business — both sites carry sameAs schema pointing at each other, and they share the same name, address, and phone. That's deliberate and good: it stitches both domains to one entity in Google's knowledge graph.

But it has one consequence that shapes everything in this document: if both sites publish pages chasing the same search query, Google splits the ranking strength between them and often ranks neither on page 1. We saw exactly this with "custom embroidery near me" split across two .net pages (both stuck on page 2) — the same thing happens across the two domains, only worse. The fix is not to compete less aggressively; it's to never compete at all. Each query class has exactly one owner, and the other site links to it.

The one-sentence version: .net wins the customer's trust and location ("who does embroidery near me?"), teamnwca wins the customer's product decision and order ("which garment, what price?") — and every page on each site hands the visitor to the other site at exactly the moment their question changes.

2. "Which site does this content belong on?" — the 30-second test

1. Is the searcher asking WHERE to get something done, or WHO to trust locally? ("near me", a city name, "embroidery shop")

→ .net — city page or service page. This is the Recovery Roadmap's core mission.

2. Is the searcher asking HOW something works, or learning? ("how does…", "what is…", "difference between fabrics")

→ .net — educational post. Your authority + 497 AI citations live here.

3. Is the searcher deciding WHICH product/style/brand to buy, or asking about PRICE? (a style number, a brand name + "custom", "best X for Y", "cost", "pricing")

→ teamnwca — product post, brand page, or tool. Don't write it on .net; link to it instead.

4. Could it honestly go either way?

→ Flag it to Erik before writing. A 2-minute check beats two pages cannibalizing each other for six months.

6. What teamnwca is doing (so nothing surprises you)

  • One product-focused blog post per week — style comparisons, brand roundups, "best X for [industry]" picks. All anchored on style numbers and catalog pages; none will touch local, service, or educational queries.
  • Brand landing pages — Carhartt and Caps are live; safety vests, polos, and New Era are next. Each is a product catalog page, not a service page — your service/city pages stay the only pages targeting service intent.
  • Product page SEO — 3,197 product pages with server-rendered titles and custom descriptions. These target style-number searches ("pc54", "richardson 112") that .net has never targeted.
  • Every teamnwca post cross-links a relevant .net explainer where one exists — that's a standing rule on our side.

7. Collision protocol — before either site writes anything new

  • Mehar, before a new .net post/page: search teamnwca.com/blog and the brand pages for the topic. If teamnwca already targets the query with buying intent → keep your angle educational/local and cross-link, or skip it. Style numbers, brand-catalog terms, "best/vs" product picks, and anything with a price → teamnwca's.
  • teamnwca (automated), before every post: we search .net's live content for the target query. If you cover it, we either change our angle to product/commercial and cross-link you, or we don't write it. This check runs automatically before every publish.
  • Ties or gray areas → Erik decides. One Slack message beats months of split rankings.

8. Shared scoreboard (monthly)

Alongside the Roadmap's metrics, we'll watch the combined picture monthly:

  • Non-brand organic clicks, both properties (Search Console) — the number that matters.
  • Quote requests / form fills from organic, whichever site they land on — it's one pipeline.
  • Cross-domain click-throughs (.net → teamnwca catalog clicks, teamnwca → .net explainer clicks).
  • Zero-cannibalization check: no query where both domains sit on page 1–3 with the same intent. If we find one, we pick an owner and 301/de-optimize the other.
  • October 2026 checkpoint — three months of curves on both sites, review together.
Bottom line: the Roadmap gives .net its mission (win local + service). teamnwca's mission is product + pricing. This guide is the treaty line between them: one owner per query, links across the border in both directions, one shared scoreboard. Follow the lane map and every page we publish makes both sites stronger.
