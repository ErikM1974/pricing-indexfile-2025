## Fabric Properties to Consider

If you’re new to [ordering custom apparel](https://nwcustomapparel.net/order-form/), understanding a few fabric basics helps you make smart choices. Let’s break down some important **fabric properties** you’ll hear about:

**Breathability** is all about how well air passes through the fabric. A breathable material lets sweat evaporate and keeps you cool, especially on busy workdays or outdoor events.

**Thread count** measures how many threads are woven together in a square inch of fabric. Higher thread counts mean tighter, denser fabric, which usually feels softer and lasts longer.

**Denier** is a way to measure the thickness of fibers in the fabric. A lower denier number means finer, lighter threads — like those found in soft, delicate fabrics. Higher denier fabrics tend to be tougher and more durable.

Then there’s the fabric’s **hand** — that’s how it feels when you touch it. Soft hands make clothes feel cozy, while rougher textures may stand up better to wear and tear.

These fabric qualities influence not just comfort but how well your logo or embroidery will hold up. For example, a dense, smooth fabric is easier to embroider neatly, while breathable, moisture-wicking fabrics help keep wearers dry and comfortable.

## How Fabric Choice Impacts Custom Embroidery & Printing Quality

Not all fabrics behave the same when embroidered or printed. Knowing which material works best can save you headaches and give you the sharpest, longest-lasting results.

For embroidery, **tightly woven fabrics like cotton or high-thread-count polyester** are ideal. The smooth surface holds the embroidery threads tightly, preventing puckering or distortion. Stretchy or very thin fabrics can cause embroidery to pull or look uneven. For embroidery-specific fabric guidance, see our **[detailed embroidery fabric selection guide](https://nwcustomapparel.net/best-fabrics-for-embroidery/)**.

While in **[screen printing or Direct-to-Garment (DTG) printing](https://nwcustomapparel.net/dtg-vs-dtf-vs-screen-printing/)**, fabric texture and density matter too. Smooth, flat surfaces give crisp, clear prints. Polyester fabrics often work well here, especially for vibrant, long-lasting designs. Fabrics with too much stretch or fuzziness can blur printed details over time.

If you’re mixing embroidery and printing, NW Custom Apparel’s experts can recommend the best fabric choices to make sure every stitch and color pops just right.

In short, understanding your customization method and your fabric’s qualities ensures your business apparel looks professional and lasts through the daily grind.

## FAQs

**What fabric is best for custom embroidery?**

The best fabric for embroidery is a tightly woven material like cotton or a high-thread-count polyester. These fabrics hold stitches well and prevent puckering or distortion. Avoid stretchy or very thin fabrics for detailed embroidery.

**Which fabrics work best for screen printing or DTG printing?**

Smooth, dense fabrics like polyester blends or cotton are ideal. They provide a flat surface that helps prints stay sharp and vibrant longer. Fuzzy or stretchy fabrics can blur or crack printed designs.

**How do I know if a fabric is breathable?**

Breathability depends on how easily air and moisture pass through the fabric. Materials like mesh or Dri-FIT polyester offer excellent breathability, keeping wearers cool and dry—great for active or outdoor workwear.

**Can I get eco-friendly fabric options for custom apparel?**

Yes! NW Custom Apparel offers fabrics like Eco-Jersey, which combines organic cotton and recycled fibers. These sustainable fabrics look great and reduce environmental impact.

**How should I care for custom embroidered or printed apparel?**

Wash custom apparel inside out in cold water and avoid harsh detergents or bleach. Hang dry or tumble dry on low to keep colors bright and embroidery intact.
