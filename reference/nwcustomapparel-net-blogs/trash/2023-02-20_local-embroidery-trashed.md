### Types Of Embroidery

Embroidery encompasses a wide range of techniques, with each style having its own unique set of characteristics. Here are some of the most [popular types of embroidery](https://nwcustomapparel.net/types-of-embroidery-techniques/):

Needlepoint embroidery is one of the oldest forms and involves stitching a design on a mesh canvas. Freestyle embroidery uses an array of colorful threads, beads, and other materials to create intricate designs without the use of a pattern. Machine embroidery utilizes automated technology to quickly stitch complex patterns onto fabric, while the counted cross stitch is created using an X-shaped stitch through the even-weave fabric.

### Materials Used In Embroidery

Creating beautiful embroidered pieces [requires the right materials](https://nwcustomapparel.net/services/custom-embroidery/). The most common materials used in embroidery include embroidery floss, thread, and cotton fabric. Embroidery floss is a strand of six to eight twisted threads that come in a wide variety of colors and textures. Embroidery thread is usually thinner than floss and comes in both silk and rayon varieties. Cotton fabric is often used as the base material, providing a sturdy surface for stitching.

In addition to these supplies, an embroiderer needs hoop frames or needlepoint yarns to keep fabrics taut while stitching. Hoop frames are metal rings that provide support as you stitch, while needlepoint yarns can be used with more delicate fabrics like tulle or fine silk.
