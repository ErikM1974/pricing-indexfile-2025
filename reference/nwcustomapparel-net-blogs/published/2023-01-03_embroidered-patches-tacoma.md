# Embroidered Patches in Tacoma: Our Local Story and Production

Walk into any job site, fire station, school event, or military base in the South Sound and you will see patches. On caps. On jackets. On uniforms. Each one tells you who someone works for, who they serve, and what they stand for.

Northwest Custom Apparel has made those patches right here since 1977. We started on Jefferson Street in Tacoma, and now operate from our production facility in Milton, minutes down the road. For nearly 50 years, we have stitched identity onto fabric for businesses, first responders, schools, and military organizations across Pierce County and beyond.

This is our story. It is also a practical guide to ordering custom embroidered patches in Tacoma today.

## How It Started: Tacoma, 1977, and the Era of the Patch Cap

When Northwest Custom Apparel first opened in Tacoma in 1977, the business had one clear mission: manufacture custom embroidered patches and apply them to caps.

![Ketchikan Alaska Fire EMS Rescue Embroidered Patch with a seaplane](https://nwcustomapparel.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/01/C2016-ketchikan-297x300-1.webp)

Ketchikan Fire Departmant

Cap styles were limited then. The dominant style was the high-crown, foam-lined cap. People called it the Elmer Fudd cap. Demand for branded versions was enormous. Companies in heavy equipment, fertilizer, construction, and agriculture recognized immediately that a branded cap was a walking advertisement. It put your logo directly in front of your target market every single day at job sites, hardware stores, trade shows, and tailgates.

A branded logo cap generates at least 4,000 impressions before it reaches the back of a closet. That math has not changed since 1977. The [ASI 2023 Ad Impressions Study](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/asi-surveys-25-000-consumers-to-prove-promos-power--effectiveness-301822772.html) surveyed nearly 25,000 consumers and confirmed branded apparel delivers recall to 85% of recipients, with apparel ranking highest of all promotional product categories.

Think about how many Caterpillar caps you still see today. That is the staying power of a well-made patch on a quality cap. These were not just promotional items. They became collectibles. Each one a small, wearable piece of local business history.

Tacoma companies like Tacoma Screw, the Propeller Club, and Tacoma Community College were among our early clients. They understood what a consistent, professional patch said about their brand. They kept coming back.

## From Elmer Fudd Caps to Trucker Hats: How Patch Culture Evolved

The patch business is not static. In the 1990s, [direct embroidery on caps](https://nwcustomapparel.net/services/custom-embroidery-caps/) became more popular than applying pre-made patches. We adapted. We invested in cap embroidery machines to meet that demand directly.

![New Era Caps styles](https://nwcustomapparel.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/11/IMG_7270-1024x735.jpeg)

But patches made a comeback. After roughly a 20-year dip, the patch cap returned to dominance. This time it was the trucker cap, a cleaner, flatter-billed descendant of the original foam-front caps we started with.

Today, the trucker cap market runs on patches. The aesthetic is sharp and intentional. A clean embroidered patch or printed patch on a structured front, worn by construction crews, coffee shop staff, and brewery workers in Fife and Milton. We carry 250+ cap styles you can pair with any patch type we make.

We produce embroidered, printed, and woven patches, each suited to different design complexity and turnaround needs. If you are deciding which type fits your artwork, our [custom embroidered patches guide](https://nwcustomapparel.net/custom-embroidered-patches/) covers the full comparison with a side-by-side breakdown.

### Why Printed Patches Changed the Game for Tacoma Businesses

One of the most significant product developments for local businesses is the printed patch. Traditional embroidered patches excel at bold logos and solid colors. Printed patches handle photographic detail, gradients, and complex multi-color artwork that embroidery threads simply cannot replicate cleanly.

At Northwest Custom Apparel, we manufacture custom printed patches and deliver them within 72 hours. If you need a rush order for a team event, a trade show, or a client gift program, printed patches are your fastest path from artwork to finished product.

For the full production process from digitizing to laser cutting, see our article on [how custom patches are made](https://nwcustomapparel.net/how-custom-patches-are-made/).

## Velcro (Hook and Loop) Backing: The Military and Tactical Choice

Organizations at Joint Base Lewis-McChord, the combined U.S. Army and Air Force installation 9 miles southwest of Tacoma, frequently require patches with hook and loop backing. The reason is practical. Unit insignias, rank patches, and morale patches on Army Combat Uniforms are swapped out regularly as assignments change. Velcro backing makes that fast and clean, with no sewing and no residue.

We supply hook and loop backed patches for JBLM units, Pierce County law enforcement, fire departments, and other organizations where patches need to be removed, replaced, or updated regularly.
