## What Custom Embroidery Is and Why It Outlasts Other Methods

### How Embroidery Works: Thread, Not Ink

Embroidery uses polyester or rayon thread stitched directly through fabric and backing material. Unlike screen printing ink or heat-transfer films, embroidered thread is structurally integrated into the garment. It does not fade, peel, crack, or wash off under normal laundering.

For work uniforms that go through 50 to 100 wash cycles over their lifetime, this structural integration is the deciding factor. A screen-printed logo on a polo shirt typically shows visible fading after 25 to 50 washes under standard commercial laundering. An embroidered logo on the same polo holds color and edge definition through the life of the garment.

**If your team wears it to work every day and washes it every week, embroider it.**

### What Digitizing Is and Why Every Order Needs It

Digitizing is the process of converting your logo from a graphic file into a stitch file. A stitch file contains machine-readable instructions that tell the embroidery machine exactly where each needle strikes, at what angle, with which stitch type, and in what sequence across each color layer.

The quality of digitizing determines the quality of the embroidered output. A poorly digitized file produces uneven fill, thread breaks, and blurred edges even on the best machine. This is why digitizing cannot be skipped or automated with basic conversion tools.

At NW Custom Apparel, digitizing is included in every embroidery order above 48 pieces at no separate charge. Once your design is digitized and stored in our machine memory, reorders run immediately with no re-digitizing cost. ### When Embroidery Is the Right Choice for Washington Workwear

Choose embroidery when:

- Your team wears uniforms daily through rain, mud, and industrial wash cycles
- You need a professional, premium appearance for client-facing staff
- Your logo is simple, bold, and under 15 thread colors
- You are decorating polos, jackets, caps, or uniform shirts

Consider screen printing or DTG when:

- You need a large full-back graphic or photographic image
- Your design has gradients, shadows, or fine detail unsuitable for stitches
- You are decorating t-shirts or performance wear for a single event
- Budget per piece is the primary constraint and longevity is secondary

For construction crews in the Pacific Northwest, embroidery on Carhartt jackets and polo shirts is the standard. The combination of thick fabric, professional appearance, and wash durability matches the demands of outdoor work in Washington weather. ## How to Order Custom Embroidery: The 5-Step Process

### Step 1: Submit Your Quote Request

Start by completing our [embroidery inquiry form](https://nwcustomapparel.net/order-form/) or calling our Milton office directly. Include:

- Your logo file (AI, EPS, PDF, or high-resolution PNG)
- Garment type and quantity
- Placement locations (left chest, cap, jacket back, sleeve)
- Deadline and any rush requirements

We review every submission within one business day.

### Step 2: Artwork Review and Digitizing

Our lead designer Steve Deland reviews your file for embroidery compatibility. He checks:

- File format and resolution
- Design complexity relative to stitch limitations
- Color count against our 15-needle capacity
- Text size (minimum 0.25 inches for legibility)

If your file needs cleanup, vectorization, or simplification, we handle it in-house and notify you of any artwork charges before proceeding.

Digitizing typically takes 1 to 2 business days for standard logos. Complex designs with specialty stitches or 3D puff may take 3 to 4 days.

### Step 3: Digital Proof and Pre-Production Sample

We create a digital mockup showing your design on the exact garment color and style. For orders over 24 pieces, we also produce a physical pre-production sample on request for your approval.

The proof includes:

- Stitch color mapping to Pantone thread matches
- Placement dimensions on the garment
- Estimated stitch count and production time

You approve the proof or request revisions. We include up to two revision rounds at no charge.

### Step 4: Production on Our 8-Head Tajima Machine

Once approved, your order enters production on our 8-head Tajima TMAR-KC Type 2. Eight heads run simultaneously, producing 8 pieces per cycle. A typical 10,000-stitch left chest logo completes approximately 8 pieces every 9 minutes.

Our DCP technology automatically adjusts presser foot pressure for different fabric weights. This means canvas work jackets and knit polo shirts can run in the same batch without manual recalibration.

### Step 5: Quality Check and Delivery

Every piece is inspected for stitch accuracy, thread color match, and trim quality. Excess threads and stabilizer material are removed. Orders are folded, boxed, and prepared for pickup at our Milton facility or shipped statewide across Washington.

Standard turnaround is 10 business days from proof approval. Rush orders available for 5-day and 3-day turnaround with additional fees.

## File Requirements: How to Prepare Your Logo for Embroidery

### Preferred File Formats

| File Type | Accepted? | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| AI / EPS / PDF (vector) | Yes — preferred | Cleanest digitizing result. Outline fonts before sending. |
| PNG / JPG (high-res raster) | Yes — accepted | 300 DPI minimum at actual print size. Transparent background preferred. |
| Low-res PNG / JPG | Case-by-case | May require recreation. Contact us before sending. |
| Word / PowerPoint | Not recommended | Low resolution. Artwork recreation likely needed. |

### Design Rules for Clean Embroidery

- **Minimum text height:** 0.25 inches (6.4mm). Smaller text blurs together.
- **Minimum line thickness:** 0.05 inches (1.27mm). Thinner lines may not stitch cleanly.
- **Maximum colors:** 15 thread colors (our machine capacity). Most logos use 3 to 7.
- **Avoid:** Gradients, photographs, fine details under 2mm, outlined or decorative fonts.
- **Simplify:** Remove extra words, dates, and phone numbers. The core mark must stand alone.

### What Happens When Your File Is Not Ready

If you send a low-resolution photo of your business card or a screenshot from your website, we do not reject your order. Our art team recreates or vectorizes the logo in-house. We notify you of any artwork charges before proceeding, so there are no surprises. See our [Custom artwork and graphic design services for apparel orders](/services/artwork/).

## Is Custom Embroidery Durable Enough for Your Work Environment?

### Wash Cycle Data and Real-World Longevity

Embroidered thread is structurally integrated into the garment. Our polyester thread maintains color through 100+ commercial wash cycles at 160°F. Screen printing ink begins to show visible degradation after 25 to 50 washes at the same temperature.

For construction crews, school kitchen staff, and healthcare workers who launder uniforms weekly, this difference determines whether a uniform program looks professional or shabby after year one.

### Pacific Northwest Weather Considerations

Washington state workwear faces rain, mud, and temperature swings. Embroidery performs better than printed alternatives in wet conditions because thread does not absorb water or lose adhesion. A screen-printed logo on a rain jacket may peel at the edges after repeated wet-dry cycles. An embroidered logo remains intact.
