## 3. Perfect Your Logo Placement

Where your **logo** appears on the jacket matters. Common spots include:

- **Chest Area:** For a professional, understated look.
- **Back:** Perfect for maximum visibility at events.
- **Sleeves:** Adds a unique touch, great for modern designs.

Keep the size proportional to the jacket. To avoid design challenges like disproportionate logos, ensure proper scaling during the design phase. Mockups are essential to visualize the placement and make adjustments for balance and visibility.

For example, A logo that’s too large can feel overwhelming, while a tiny logo might go unnoticed. Test out a few placements with mockups to find the best fit.

## 5. Follow a Step-by-Step Design Process

### Quick Checklist for Design

- **Step 1:** Brainstorm designs with team input. Start with sketches or brainstorm sessions. Gather input from your team to ensure the design aligns with your company’s goals. Think about how the jacket will be worn at events, in the office, or during outdoor work.
- **Step 2:** Create digital mockups with brand colors, logos, and patterns using tools like Adobe Illustrator or Canva. These mockups allow you to experiment with **colors, logo placement, and additional details** without committing to physical samples.
- **Step 3:** Produce a physical prototype to check fit, feel, and overall design. Work with your manufacturer to create a sample jacket and make adjustments as needed before moving to full production.
- **Step 4:** Finalize and place your bulk production order.

## FAQs

**How can I make my jacket designs more unique?**

You can make your custom jackets distinctive by adding elements such as custom linings, embroidered patches, or personalized zipper pulls. Unique design features help reflect your brand's personality while adding a creative touch.

**How do I decide the best color scheme for my jackets?**

Choose colors that align with your brand’s identity and evoke the right emotions. For instance, blue conveys trust, while red symbolizes energy. Consider using a digital mockup to test how your brand colors work together on the jacket.
