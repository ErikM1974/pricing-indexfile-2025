## What Carhartt Actually Is (and Which Product Line Fits Your Crew)

Carhartt started in 1889 when Hamilton Carhartt opened a small factory in Detroit. He made heavy duck canvas overalls for railroad workers. That same brown duck canvas still sits at the heart of the brand today.

The company now runs two distinct product lines. Understanding the split matters for procurement:

![Custom workwear Carhartt jacket](https://nwcustomapparel.net/wp-content/uploads/2025/02/carhartt-jacket.webp)

**Carhartt Core** handles industrial workwear. Think flame-resistant jackets, bib overalls, and the brown duck canvas that construction crews in Seattle and Bellevue rely on through rainy winters. This line includes union-made options at facilities in Kentucky and Tennessee.

**Carhartt WIP (Work In Progress)** launched in 1994 as a European fashion line. Slimmer fits, trendier cuts, higher prices. A standard Carhartt Chore Coat runs about $100. The WIP version costs closer to $198.

For Washington state buyers, the decision is straightforward. Core line for job sites. WIP for front-desk staff or retail teams who need the look without the ANSI compliance requirements. We see this split constantly at our Milton shop. A concrete contractor from Olympia needs triple-stitched seams. A coffee roaster in Ballard wants the aesthetic on a slimmer jacket. Same brand, different purpose.

## Is Carhartt Worth the Price? A 2026 Evaluation

The global workwear market is growing at roughly 5% annually. Carhartt's popularity is not a flash trend. The brand has structural advantages that justify the premium for bulk buyers.

**Durability validation:** Amazon review analysis shows "durable material" appears in nearly 60 percent of positive Carhartt reviews. The most common complaint is sizing inconsistency, not quality failure.

**Workforce perception:** Employees actually want to wear Carhartt. The brand carries cultural weight from hip-hop adoption in the 1990s to modern streetwear credibility. That translates to higher uniform compliance. Less PPE resistance. Fewer "forgot my jacket" excuses on cold mornings.

**Resale value:** Vintage Carhartt Detroit Jackets sell for $150 to $400 depending on era and condition. The brand launched Carhartt Reworked in 2023, a trade-in program that cleans and resells used gear. This circularity matters for buyers with sustainability mandates.

**Union and domestic manufacturing:** Carhartt employs 2,700 workers in the United States. Roughly 970 are United Food and Commercial Workers union members. The company operates three U.S. manufacturing facilities and maintains a "Made in the USA" collection according to [the official Carhartt company history page](https://www.carhartt.com/carhartt-history). For Washington state agencies and federal contractors, this domestic production counts toward procurement preference points.

The Carhartt wearer has evolved from a single demographic into a diverse global community. Traditionally, the brand belonged to the Maker Class—the welders, carpenters, and farmers who required **triple-stitched seams** and tool pockets. These users value the brand for its utility and safety.

Today, the demographic includes:

- **The Skilled Trades**: Professionals who view their gear as a tool for survival in harsh weather.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is Carhartt WIP the same as regular Carhartt?**

No. Carhartt WIP (Work In Progress) is a separate fashion line with slimmer fits and higher prices. Core Carhartt handles industrial durability and safety compliance.

**Is Carhartt still made in the USA?**

Select products are. The company operates three U.S. facilities and offers a "Made in the USA" collection. Other lines are produced in Mexico and Europe to meet global demand.

**Why does Carhartt have a strong odor?**

New duck canvas carries a natural smell from the waxed treatment. It fades after a few washes. Some buyers air out garments before first wear.

**How long does a Carhartt jacket last?**

With regular job site use, eight to ten years is common. We see clients bring jackets back for logo updates at year six or seven. The fabric outlasts the branding.

**Can you embroider on Carhartt duck canvas?**

Yes. The 12oz canvas is ideal for embroidery. The tight weave holds stitches without puckering. We use proper backing and 3.5mm stitch density for clean results.

**Why is Carhartt considered so durable?**

Carhartt uses heavy-duty fabrics, reinforced stitching, and rugged construction techniques to ensure durability. Their duck canvas and triple-stitched seams make garments resistant to wear and tear, making them last for years.

**Why do celebrities wear Carhartt?**

Celebrities appreciate Carhartt’s authenticity and timeless appeal. It represents a blend of rugged utility and effortless street style, making it popular among musicians, skaters, and influencers.
