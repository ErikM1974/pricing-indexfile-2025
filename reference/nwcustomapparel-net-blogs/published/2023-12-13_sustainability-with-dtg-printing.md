The Most Sustainable Choice: Kornit DTG Printing with Zero Chemical Waste

## The System: Why Kornit DTG Eliminates Chemical Waste

The central question in sustainable printing is: **which direct to garment print systems are most eco-friendly?** The answer lies in the hardware's core process and chemistry. Our investment in Kornit technology is not merely a preference; it is a structural commitment to eliminating waste.

### The Core Technology: NeoPigment™ Water-Based Inks

Kornit’s patented **[NeoPigment™ inks](https://www.kornit.com/)** stand apart from conventional apparel inks. They are water-based, non-toxic, and free of heavy metals, formaldehyde, and alkylphenol ethoxylates (APE).

- **Verifiable Safety:** These inks meet the strict safety standards required for infant wear, earning certifications like **[OEKO-TEX Standard 100](https://www.oeko-tex.com/en/label-check/)** and being compliant with the Global Organic Textile Standard (GOTS-5). This provides customers with concrete proof that the finished apparel is safe for all ages and the environment.

### Zero Wastewater: The Closed-Loop Process

![Young Man loading a t-shirt on the Kornit Direct To Garment Printer. The ink on the shirt is water-based an sustainable](https://nwcustomapparel.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/12/Brian-Loading-the-Kornit-DTG-Printer-1024x768.jpg)

Traditional decoration methods rely on chemicals for setup and cleanup, creating wastewater that requires complex disposal. The Kornit system eliminates this entirely:

- **No External Pre-Treatment:** The pre-treatment chemicals needed for dark garments are applied and integrated directly into the machine's printing cycle. There is no need for external equipment, post-print washing, or extra steps that introduce water and chemical runoff.
- **Zero Chemical Waste:** Kornit’s innovative approach means the entire printing process, from design to finished shirt, is contained. As CEO **Jim Mickelson** observed, the process generates **“absolutely no chemical waste,”** which is a significant shift from the industry's chemical-intensive past.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**What are the best sustainable garments for DTG printing?**

The best choices are shirts made from 100% organic cotton or high-quality blends that include recycled polyester or ethically sourced rayon (like tri-blends). These fabrics offer the optimal surface for our water-based inks while ensuring responsible material sourcing.

**Are Kornit NeoPigment™ inks certified by any environmental body?**

Yes. Kornit NeoPigment™ inks have achieved OEKO-TEX Standard 100 certification, which confirms that they are free from harmful substances and are safe for human ecological use. They also comply with the GOTS-5 standard, critical for organic fabric printing.

**How much water is saved using DTG compared to traditional screen printing?**

The water savings are substantial. Because our DTG system requires no water for cleaning screens or washing off external pre-treatment chemicals, the entire water consumption related to production cleanup is eliminated, which saves thousands of gallons annually compared to water-intensive screen printing operations.

**Is the DTG pre-treatment process eco-friendly?**

Yes. The pre-treatment used in our Kornit system is applied during the print cycle and is non-toxic. Unlike methods that require washing the garment after pre-treatment, our closed-loop process integrates the solution into the fabric and cures it instantly, making it a sustainable and necessary step for achieving vibrant colors on dark apparel.

**What makes a shirt sustainable for custom printing?**

A sustainable shirt is defined by its material, manufacturing, and lifespan. It often uses recycled or organic content, involves low water usage during growth and production, and is made by a brand with ethical labor standards. We prioritize garments that meet these criteria so your custom environmentally friendly apparel truly reflects your values.
