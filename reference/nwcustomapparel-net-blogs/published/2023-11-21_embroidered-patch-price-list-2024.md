## How Much Do Custom Embroidered Patches Cost?

The **cost of custom embroidered patches** varies depending on several key factors such as size, quantity, design complexity, and the type of backing you choose. Here’s a detailed breakdown to help you understand the pricing dynamics and make informed decisions for your custom patch projects:

### 1. Size and Shape of the Patch

The size of your patch is a primary cost driver. Larger patches require more fabric, thread, and stitching time which increases the price. For example, a 1-inch patch is more affordable than a 6-inch one. Additionally, custom shapes may incur extra costs due to specialized cutting processes.

### 2. Quantity Ordered

Ordering in bulk can lead to substantial savings. Our pricing structure offers discounts as the order quantity increases. For example, ordering 100 patches reduces the per-patch cost compared to ordering 25.

### 3. Embroidery Coverage

The amount of embroidery (measured as a percentage of the patch surface covered) affects the price. Patches with 50% coverage are less expensive than those with 100% coverage due to the amount of thread and labor involved.

- **50% Coverage:** Ideal for simple logos with a lot of background fabric visible.
- **75% Coverage:** Common for mid-range designs that require more detail.
- **100% Coverage:** Perfect for intricate designs but the most expensive due to increased thread use.

### Design Complexity

Intricate designs with fine details, gradients, or multiple colors are more expensive because they take more stitching time and specialized digitization to produce. Simpler logos or text-based designs are more cost-effective.

### Thread Colors

NW Custom Apparel includes up to 9 thread colors in their base price. Using additional colors can raise costs, especially for designs requiring metallic or glow-in-the-dark threads.

### Backing Options

The backing type determines how a patch attaches to fabric such as pressure-sensitive, low melt, or heat seal and can affects the price. Each backing type offers different application methods and durability levels.

- **Iron-On:** Affordable and easy to apply, making it popular for DIY projects.
- **Sew-On:** Durable but requires manual labor, often chosen for uniforms or heavy-duty use.
- **Velcro:** Higher costs due to the dual-layer backing but ideal for removable patches.
- **Adhesive:** A budget-friendly option for temporary uses like event giveaways.

### Additional Features

Special options like metallic threads, extra colors beyond the standard six, or Velcro backing can add to the base price. For instance, metallic thread adds 25% to the base price, and Velcro backing adds another 25%.

### Material Choices

The choice of fabric and thread can significantly affect patch pricing. Twill is a popular base fabric due to its durability and cost-effectiveness. Felt is another option but can be pricier for large designs. Polyester threads are standard, offering vibrant colors at a reasonable cost, while specialty threads like rayon or metallic threads add a premium.

### How to Calculate Your Patch Size

Determining the size of your patch is simple:  
*Size = (Width + Height) / 2*  
Measure the widest and tallest point of your desired patch, add them together, and divide by two to get the standard size for pricing.
