# How to Order Custom Hoodies for Your Business: A Buyer's Guide

## Why Hoodies Work for Business Branding

A hoodie is the most-worn garment in any company uniform program. Employees grab it first on cold mornings. Clients remember it at trade shows. Teams wear it year-round.

We've produced custom hoodies for Washington businesses since 1977. Through thousands of production runs at our Milton, WA facility, we've learned what fabric weights hold up, which decoration methods last, and where most orders go wrong before they start.

This guide covers what to know before you order; not generic advice from a marketing team that has never stood in front of a heat press.

## Fabric Weight: The Decision Most Buyers Get Wrong

Fabric determines everything: how your logo looks, how long the garment lasts, and whether your team actually wears it. The most common post-delivery complaint we hear is fit and feel, not decoration quality.

Hoodies come in three weight categories. Choosing the wrong one for your use case is expensive to fix after production.

| Weight | GSM Range | Best For | Decoration Method |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Lightweight | 180–220 | Layering, indoor events, warm climates | Screen print, DTG |
| Midweight | 240–280 | Daily workwear, team uniforms, corporate gifts | Embroidery, screen print |
| Heavyweight | 300–400 | Outdoor crews, construction, cold-weather outer layer | Embroidery (preferred), screen print |

Most Washington businesses need midweight fleece. It provides enough structure for clean embroidery without the bulk that makes screen-printed designs feel stiff. Heavyweight hoodies work best for construction and trade crews who wear them as outer layers in rain and cold.

## Four Ways to Print Hoodies - At a Glance

Not every method works on every hoodie. We've fixed enough failed orders to know where the breakdown happens. Here's what each method does, and when we recommend it.

### Embroidery

Stitched thread becomes part of the garment structure. It does not crack, peel, or fade through 100+ wash cycles. We recommend embroidery for hoodies that see daily wear — construction crews, uniform programs, and any garment that goes through commercial laundry.

Our default choice for workwear. [See our embroidered hoodie service for stitch counts, fleece weight specs, and pricing →](https://nwcustomapparel.net/services/custom-embroidered-sweatshirts-hoodies/)

### Screen Printing

Ink pushed through a mesh screen produces the most vivid, opaque color of any method. Best for large back prints, team names, and event graphics with 3–6 colors. Minimum order is 24 pieces.

Our choice for bold graphics at volume. [See our screen-printed hoodie service for brand catalog, print placement options, and turnaround →](https://nwcustomapparel.net/services/screen-printing/custom-hoodies/)

### DTG Printing

Our Kornit Storm Hexa printer handles photographic designs and unlimited colors without screen setup. No minimum order. 48-hour turnaround on open slots.

Best for small runs, photo-realistic designs, and samples. [See our direct-to-garment printing service →](https://nwcustomapparel.net/direct-to-garment/)

### DTF Printing

Heat-transferred film with adhesive powder. Works on cotton, polyester, nylon, and all blends — the only method with no fabric restrictions.

Best for polyester athletic hoodies and mixed-fabric orders. [See our direct-to-film printing service →](https://nwcustomapparel.net/services/direct-to-film-printing/)

## What to Know Before You Order

### Sizing: Hoodies Run Large

Hoodies fit larger than T-shirts. A medium T-shirt wearer typically needs a small hoodie. For bulk orders, we recommend this distribution: 10% small, 40% medium, 35% large, 15% XL.

Construction crews tend to size up for layering. Tech teams prefer fitted cuts. We provide manufacturer size charts before you order. For [company uniform programs](https://nwcustomapparel.net/company-uniform-program-guide/) with 50+ employees, we recommend sampling 2–3 sizes on your actual team before the bulk run.

### Artwork and File Prep

Embroidery requires a digitized stitch file — not a JPEG or PNG. We handle [logo digitizing](https://nwcustomapparel.net/logo-digitizing-for-embroidery/) in-house on Pulse DG/ML software. Turnaround is 24–48 hours for standard logos.

Screen printing and DTG accept vector files (AI, EPS, PDF) or high-resolution PNGs. DTG can print directly from photographic images at 300 DPI minimum.

### Sampling Before Production

We produce a physical pre-production sample on your actual garment for approval before any bulk run over 48 pieces. This catches color accuracy, placement, and sizing issues before they affect your entire order. There is no substitute for holding the actual garment in your hand.

## Common Questions About Custom Hoodie Orders

**Can I mix embroidery and screen printing on the same hoodie?**

Yes. A standard combination is embroidered left-chest logo plus screen-printed full-back team name. We handle mixed-decoration orders under one production approval and one delivery.

**How do I choose between midweight and heavyweight fleece?**

Midweight (240–280 GSM) works for 80% of business orders. It layers comfortably and accepts all decoration methods. Choose heavyweight (300+ GSM) only for outdoor crews who wear hoodies as outer layers in cold weather. Lightweight (180–220 GSM) is rarely appropriate for workwear — it lacks the structure for clean embroidery and wears out faster.

**What file format do I need for my logo?**

Embroidery requires a digitized DST or PES file. We create this from your vector logo or high-resolution image. Screen printing and DTG accept vector files or 300 DPI PNGs. We review every file before production and flag issues — like a 72 DPI screenshot that will print as a pixelated blob.

**Will my hoodie decoration fade or crack?**

Embroidery does not fade or crack. It outlasts the garment. Screen printing lasts 50+ washes with proper care (cold wash, tumble dry low). DTG and DTF are durable but slightly less resistant to heavy abrasion than screen print. For workwear that sees commercial laundry, embroidery is the safest long-term choice.

**Can you ship hoodies to multiple locations?**

Yes. For businesses with distributed teams, we ship individual packages direct to employees. For [company webstore](https://nwcustomapparel.net/services/webstores/) clients, employees log in, select their size, and we handle fulfillment automatically — no administrative burden on your HR team.
