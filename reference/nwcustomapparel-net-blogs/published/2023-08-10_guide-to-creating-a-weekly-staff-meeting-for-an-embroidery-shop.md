## Building Accountability Through Regular Touchpoints

At Northwest Custom Apparel, we believe accountability is the engine that drives our merit-based pay system, and it all starts with answering the fundamental question every employee has: "How am I doing?" We don’t wait for an annual review to answer this. Instead, we utilize our team meetings as active touchpoints to clarify expectations and review specific "Action Plans." By discussing quantifiable goals—whether it’s production speed or sales figures—during these gatherings, we eliminate surprises. This constant communication ensures that when it’s time for compensation reviews, our high performers know precisely why they are receiving top-tier raises. Those needing improvement have already received the immediate feedback required to get back on track.

## Individual Staff Reports

Let's focus on individual reports, where each team member shares updates on their current projects and progress. You have your own tasks, so communicating your progress is essential. You're not isolated; you're part of a team, and your contributions matter.

When you share your updates, you're not just reporting on your tasks; you're helping the team gauge the overall project's progress. You're sharing your challenges, your wins, and your insights. It's about transparency and collaboration.

## Sales Topics During Your Weekly Embroidery Shop Meeting

You're now moving on to discuss sales topics during your meetings. This is where you'll review your team's sales performance, discuss new strategies, and address any challenges you face.

You'll want to highlight top performers and share their successful tactics with the team. It's also critical to address any areas where the team could improve. Are you meeting your sales targets? If not, what's holding you back? Perhaps there's a need for additional training or resources.
