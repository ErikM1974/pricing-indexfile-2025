# The ROI of Custom T-Shirts: Cost Per Impression and Fabric Quality

Custom T-shirts remain one of the most popular and cost-effective ways to promote a brand and achieve maximum market exposure. When procurement managers view T-shirts as a marketing asset rather than a simple expense, the return on investment becomes clear.

## The Financial Argument: Cost Per Impression (CPI)

T-shirts are a highly efficient form of mobile advertising that generates strong impressions in your market.

Consider an average cost for a high-volume **screen-printed T-shirt** to be around $8.00. This single item is a long-term advertising medium:

- **Duration:** A T-shirt lasts approximately six months.
- **Exposure:** If worn just twice a month, the shirt can be seen by anywhere from 10 to 200 persons per wear, depending on the location.
- **Total Impressions:** Over six months, a single shirt can generate as many as **3,600 impressions**.

This translates to an advertising **Cost Per Impression** of approximately **1 cent to 13.3 cents**. Compared to the high cost of digital ads or billboards, custom apparel provides an exceptional bang for your buck.

## The Quality Argument: Understanding Fabric Choices

The long-term success and branding value of a T-shirt depends heavily on its fabric. There are three primary choices when manufacturing cotton-based apparel, each suitable for a different end use.

### Ring Spun Cotton

**Ring spun cotton** is the highest quality fabric choice. The cotton fibers undergo a spinning process during manufacturing, which makes the individual fibers longer and significantly softer. This process removes the rough texture of raw cotton, producing a garment with superior fit and feel.

- **Best Use:** High-end retail, corporate gifts, and apparel where comfort and quality are the priority. Manufacturers like **[Cutter and Buck](https://nwcustomapparel.net/cutter-and-buck-wholesale/)** feature ring spun polo shirts in their premium product line.

### Regular Cotton (Carded Open End)

**Regular cotton** is produced by carding the raw cotton and twisting the fibers into a yarn. The yarn is then woven into the final material.

- **Best Use:** This material is an **industry staple** for promotional tees, fun runs, and large-quantity giveaway shirts. It offers breathability and a lower manufacturing cost, making it ideal for large-scale events that require a functional, affordable product.

## The Expert Argument: Decoration Compatibility

The fabric you select must be compatible with the final decoration method for optimal durability and appearance.

- **Screen Printing:** Both Regular Cotton and Ring Spun Cotton accept traditional plastisol and water-based inks well. However, **50/50 shirts** are notably difficult to print using water-based inks due to the polyester content. **[The Screen Printing Service](https://nwcustomapparel.net/services/screen-printing/)** provides details on ink suitability for various fibers.
- **[DTG Printing](https://nwcustomapparel.net/direct-to-garment/):** This method relies heavily on cotton fibers for color vibrancy and permanence. **Ring Spun Cotton** provides the best, smoothest surface for high-resolution DTG printing, ensuring the image is crisp and professional.

When you invest in a quality T-shirt, you ensure the advertisement lasts longer and reflects positively on your brand.

## FAQs

**How many colors can I use on a T-shirt before the cost significantly increases?**

The cost structure depends entirely on the decoration method. For screen printing, the cost increases significantly with each color added because a new screen must be made and set up for every color. We generally recommend limiting designs to four colors or less for cost efficiency in high-volume orders. In contrast, for embroidery or Direct-to-Garment printing, the number of colors usually has minimal to no impact on the price.

**Does the wash process affect the ROI of a T-shirt?**

Yes, the wash process is crucial to the shirt's longevity and, therefore, its ROI. Ring Spun Cotton holds up better over time than cheaper cotton. More importantly, screen-printed designs should be washed inside-out in cold water to prevent cracking and fading. Poor washing habits reduce the T-shirt's lifespan, decreasing the total number of impressions and increasing your effective CPI.

**Which decoration method is best for achieving photo-realistic images?**

For complex designs, gradients, or photo-realistic images, Direct-to-Garment printing is the superior method. DTG functions like a large inkjet printer, allowing for an unlimited color palette and high-resolution detail that screen printing (which uses separate screens for each color) cannot match. If your branding requires detailed imagery, choose a high-quality, Ring Spun cotton shirt and the DTG technique.

**What is the standard minimum order quantity (MOQ) for custom T-shirts?**

The MOQ varies significantly by the chosen decoration method. For screen printing, the MOQ is typically higher (often 12 to 24 shirts) because of the high initial setup cost (making the screens). For embroidery or DTG printing, the MOQ can be much lower or even zero, as the setup process is digital and faster. Always check the MOQ based on your desired printing technique.

**What is "color migration" and why is it a concern with 50/50 shirts?**

Color migration is a technical issue where the dye from a polyester shirt fabric bleeds into the ink of the printed design, causing the image color to shift (often giving it a reddish tint). This is a risk with 50/50 poly/cotton blend shirts and pure polyester items. To prevent this, professional screen printers use special low-bleed inks and monitor curing temperatures carefully. If you use a 50/50 blend, ensure your supplier uses anti-migration printing techniques.
