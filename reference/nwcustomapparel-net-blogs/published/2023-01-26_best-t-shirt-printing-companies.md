## What to Look for Before Choosing a Custom T-Shirt Printing Company

Smart buyers evaluate decorators against criteria that affect production quality, cost, and delivery. These six criteria separate professional bulk decorators from consumer-facing print-on-demand platforms.

**In-house vs outsourced production.** Companies that decorate in-house control quality directly. They catch errors before shipping. They adjust production in real time. National print-on-demand companies often route orders to fulfillment partners. Quality varies by location. Accountability diffuses across a network. Ask every company directly: who prints the shirts?

**Minimum order quantities.** Consumer services advertise "no minimums" but charge premium per-unit pricing. A B2B bulk order of 50 to 500+ units needs volume pricing tiers. Screen printing becomes cost-effective at 24 pieces. Embroidery starts at 6 pieces. DTG and DTF carry no minimums but cost more per piece at volume. Understand the MOQ structure before requesting a quote.

For large cotton and cotton-blend orders, we recommend **[bulk screen printing for Washington State business orders](https://nwcustomapparel.net/services/screen-printing/)** starting at 24 pieces with volume discounts at 100 and 250 units.

![Line graph comparing the cost efficiency of Screen Printing versus DTG printing across different order volumes.](https://nwcustomapparel.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/01/t-shirt-printing-cost-efficiency-chart-1024x572.webp)

Screen printing offers significant economies of scale, while DTG is ideal for testing small batches without setup fees.

**Decoration method availability.** A company that only screen prints cannot produce embroidered polo shirts for your office staff. A company that only does DTG will bleed money on a 200-piece event shirt order. Look for multi-method capability under one roof. Screen printing, embroidery, DTG, DTF, heat transfer, and laser engraving should all be available from a single vendor like Northwest Custom Apparel for complex uniform programs.

When your order falls below 24 pieces or contains photographic detail, **[DTG printing for small runs and complex designs](https://nwcustomapparel.net/direct-to-garment/)** handles unlimited colors with no minimum quantity.

**Turnaround time from your location.** A national company quotes "7 business day production" from its facility in Kansas or Florida. Add 3 to 5 days of UPS Ground transit to Washington State. The actual delivery timeline stretches to 10 to 12 days. A local decorator's "7 business day production" means 7 days to your door if you pick up, or plus one day for local delivery. For rush orders, geography wins.

**Artwork and proofing support.** Can you call and speak to a human about your logo? Will you receive a digital proof before production? Budget online-only services provide minimal proofing. A local print shop reviews your file, flags issues, and sends a mock-up before burning screens or digitizing stitches.

**Track record with your industry.** A company that regularly serves construction companies, schools, and corporate teams in Washington understands safety apparel requirements, school spirit wear size runs, and bulk corporate uniform consistency. A national fulfillment center processes orders from a template. It does not carry regional industry knowledge by default.

Screen printing delivers the lowest per-unit cost for bulk business orders at 24 pieces or more. The setup cost amortizes across the full order quantity. At 100+ pieces, screen printing costs significantly less per shirt than DTG, DTF, or embroidery. **What is the difference between screen printing and DTG for t-shirts?**

Screen printing pushes ink through a mesh screen onto fabric. It produces vivid, opaque color and lasts 25 to 50+ commercial wash cycles. It requires a minimum of 24 pieces and a separate screen per color. DTG sprays water-based ink directly onto cotton fibers. It handles unlimited colors and photographic detail with no minimum order. It lasts 20 to 40 wash cycles. Screen printing wins on cost at volume. DTG wins on flexibility for small runs.

**What file format do I need to submit for custom t-shirt printing?**

Submit vector files (AI, EPS, or print-ready PDF) for screen printing and embroidery. Vector files scale without resolution loss and separate cleanly into color layers. High-resolution PNG or JPG at 300 DPI minimum at actual print size are accepted for DTG and DTF. Convert all text to outlines. Specify Pantone colors for screen print and thread colors for embroidery.
