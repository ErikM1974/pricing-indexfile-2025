## Why This Order Worked: Three Systems Operating Together

Same-day DTG is not luck. Our ability to provide **[Same-Day t-shirt Service](https://nwcustomapparel.net/rush-t-shirts/)** relies on the intersection of three systems that remove delay at every stage.

### System 1 - Digital File Processing That Takes Minutes, Not Hours

Traditional screen printing requires film output, screen burning, ink mixing, and press registration. That setup alone takes 2–4 hours. DTG replaces all of it with digital workflow.

Our RIP (Raster Image Processor) software converts your design file into printer-ready format automatically. It calculates white underbase density for dark garments. It adjusts color profiles for fabric type. What used to require a prepress technician now happens in software in under 15 minutes.

**What this means for your rush order:** Send us a high-resolution PNG or PDF. If the file is clean, we are printing within 20 minutes of approval. No screen burning. No color mixing. No registration delays.

### System 3 - Industrial Equipment That Prints 30+ Dark Shirts Per Hour

Speed without reliability is worthless. Our Kornit industrial DTG printers are the difference between "we can try" and "we can guarantee."

- **Integrated pretreatment:** Applied inside the machine, not sprayed manually. Eliminates a 5-minute-per-shirt bottleneck.
- **Dual printheads:** White underbase and color layers print simultaneously in one pass. Cuts production time in half versus single-head desktop systems.
- **Recirculating white ink:** Prevents pigment settling and nozzle clogging. Older printers stop every 20–30 shirts for maintenance. Our systems run continuously.

Desktop DTG printers produce 6–8 dark shirts per hour. Our industrial Kornit systems produce 30+. This speed proves the value of our machinery, which is why we previously detailed the value of [**industrial DTG efficiency and speed** over desktop alternatives](https://nwcustomapparel.net/industrial-dtg-speed-production-cost/). This speed and the automation of the entire process allowed us to print, inspect, and pack the Marine corporals’ shirts by the 5 PM deadline. That is the difference between a same-day delivery and a same-week delivery.

## This is How a DTG Printer Works

The Kornit printers we use are the best for DTG. They create an outstanding print in a short amount of time. Our excellent staff and the techy machines produce a great shirt. A computer attaches to the printer to load files and apply settings. However, this only scratches the surface of the machine's operation.

A pneumatic vacuum system powered by a compressor moves the ink through the machine and onto the shirt. It also sprays the t-shirt inside the printer for a simple pre-treatment process. The pallets and rails use magnets to glide back and forth during printing. The built-in heaters inside the print orifices keep them at the right temperature.

A humidifier inside the machine ensures the ink is moist and doesn't dry up before hitting the shirt. In addition, there are plenty of pumps, filters, and tubes. These create a complex unison of tech that allows the machine to do its job. More importantly, it gives the customer the shirt they want.
