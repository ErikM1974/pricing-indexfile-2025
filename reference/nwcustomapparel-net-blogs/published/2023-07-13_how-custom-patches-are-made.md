# How Custom Patches Are Made at Northwest Custom Apparel

## From Artwork to Finished Patch

A custom patch looks simple. A design, some thread, a border. But the production process involves five distinct stages, each requiring specialized equipment and trained operators. A mistake at any stage produces a patch that frays, distorts, or fails to match the approved design.

At Northwest Custom Apparel in Milton, Washington, we have produced custom embroidered patches since 1977. We have manufactured patches for Boeing, military units at Joint Base Lewis-McChord, fire departments across Pierce County, and businesses throughout the Seattle-Tacoma area. We have refined our process over decades. This article covers exactly how we turn your design into a finished patch.

## Step 1. Design and Digitizing

### Artwork Requirements

The process begins with your design file. We require vector files in AI, EPS, or PDF format. Raster files (JPEG, PNG) cannot be scaled for production and cannot be digitized for embroidery.

If your design includes text, we recommend fonts with consistent stroke weight and minimal fine detail. For guidance on designing logos that work on patches, see our article on [logo design principles for apparel patches](https://nwcustomapparel.net/logo-design-principles-apparel-patches/).

### Patch Digitizing Process

Digitizing is the most skilled step in patch production. A trained operator uses Wilcom EmbroideryStudio software to convert your vector design into a stitch file. This file tells the embroidery machine exactly where to place each stitch, in what direction, at what density, and when to change thread colors.

The digitizer makes critical decisions:

- **Stitch path:** The sequence in which the machine sews each element. Efficient pathing reduces thread breaks and production time.
- **Stitch density:** How closely stitches are packed. High density on small patches causes fabric distortion. Low density on large patches looks sparse.
- **Underlay pattern:** Foundation stitches that stabilize the fabric and prevent puckering.
- **Color sequencing:** Minimizing thread changes reduces production time and cost.

A complex patch with 12,000 stitches takes 2 to 4 hours to digitize. A simple text patch takes 30 minutes. The one-time digitizing fee is included in your setup cost. We store the stitch file for reorders.

For more on digitizing, see our [logo digitizing for embroidery](https://nwcustomapparel.net/logo-digitizing-for-embroidery/) service page.

## Step 2. Patch Production on Multi-Head Embroidery Machines

### Hooping and Fabric Setup

Once the stitch file is approved, we load the base fabric onto the embroidery machine. For standard patches, we use twill or felt backing. The fabric is hooped (stretched in a rigid frame) to maintain tension during stitching.

We select thread colors from our inventory of approximately 250 Madeira and Isacord threads. We match your Pantone codes to the closest available thread. If you require an exact brand color match, we can special-order thread at additional cost and lead time.

### Machine Operation

Our Tajima TMAR-KC 8-head embroidery machine runs at 1,100 RPM (revolutions per minute). Each head has 15 needles, allowing us to run complex multi-color designs without frequent thread changes.

The machine stitches the design onto the base fabric in panels. Multiple patches are produced simultaneously on a single panel. A 12,000-stitch patch takes approximately 8 to 12 minutes per head to complete.

[![Beige caps stitching](https://nwcustomapparel.net/wp-content/uploads/2015/10/Cap-Embroidery-Machine-1024x576.png)](https://nwcustomapparel.net/wp-content/uploads/2015/10/Cap-Embroidery-Machine.png)

During production, our operator monitors:

- Thread tension consistency
- Color accuracy against the approved sample
- Needle breaks or thread jams
- Placement alignment on each panel

We do not leave machines unattended during patch runs. Quality control happens during production, not just after.

## Step 3. Backing Application

After stitching, the patches are still part of a larger fabric panel. We apply backing to give the patch structure and a method of attachment.

### Backing Types

| Backing Type | Best For | Application Method | Durability |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Sew-on | Uniforms, dress garments, permanent attachment | Sew directly to fabric | Highest |
| Heat seal | Caps, jackets, workwear | Industrial heat press | High |
| Iron-on | Light fabrics, casual use, home application | Standard household iron | Moderate (20–30 washes) |
| Velcro / hook and loop | Military, tactical, JBLM units, removable patches | Press or sew loop side to garment | Very high |
| Plastic | Collectible caps, display items, shape retention | Sew or adhesive | Shape-retaining |

We apply backing using a fusing machine that bonds the material to the patch panel with heat and pressure. The backing type you choose affects the patch's longevity and how it attaches to your garment.

For military and tactical organizations at Joint Base Lewis-McChord, we regularly produce [hook and loop backed patches](https://nwcustomapparel.net/services/custom-patch/) for ACU uniforms. These patches swap out cleanly as unit assignments change.

## Step 4. Cutting and Shaping

### Laser Cutting for Precision

We use a laser cutter to separate individual patches from the stitched panels. The laser cuts intricate shapes with clean edges and sealed perimeters. This is faster and more accurate than traditional mechanical die cutting.

The laser can follow complex outlines: state shapes, animal silhouettes, logos with protrusions. For standard geometric shapes (circles, ovals, squares, shields), we can also use merrowed borders after cutting.

### Border Finishing

After cutting, we apply the border that frames the patch and prevents fraying.

- **Merrowed border:** An overlock stitch wraps around the patch edge. Slightly raised, traditional, highly durable. Best for standard shapes.
- **Satin border:** A flat, clean edge created by the laser cutting process. Modern, precise. Best for complex die-cut shapes.

The border choice is determined during the design phase. Merrowed borders require standard shapes. Satin borders work with any shape.

## Step 5. Quality Inspection and Finishing

### Individual Inspection

Every patch is inspected before packaging. We check for:

- Thread tension consistency across the design
- Color accuracy against the approved sample
- Clean edges with no fraying or loose threads
- Correct dimensions and shape
- Backing adhesion integrity

Patches that fail inspection are pulled and replaced. We do not ship defective patches.

### Packaging and Delivery

Approved patches are counted, sorted, and packaged. Bulk orders are packed by size or design variant. Individual name orders are packed separately. We ship via UPS Ground with tracking. Local pickup is available at our Milton, WA facility.

Standard turnaround for embroidered patches is 10 business days. Rush orders are available for 5-day turnaround when our schedule allows. [Printed patches](https://nwcustomapparel.net/services/custom-patch/) can be produced in 72 hours for urgent needs.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How long does patch production take?**

Standard embroidered patches: 10 business days. Rush: 5 business days. Printed patches: 72 hours. Leather and PVC patches: 2 to 3 weeks.

**What is the minimum order?**

100 patches for embroidered and woven. No minimum for printed patches. Leather and PVC have higher minimums due to material sourcing.

**Can you match my exact brand colors?**

We match Pantone Solid Coated codes to our thread inventory. For exact matches, we can special-order thread. Specify your Pantone codes when you submit your design.

**What file format should I send?**

You must send vector files such as AI, EPS, or PDF with fonts outlined. High-resolution PNG at 300 DPI is acceptable but requires more cleanup.

**Do you offer Made in USA patches?**

Yes. NW Custom Apparel produces patches in-house at our Milton, WA facility. We also offer overseas production for large volume orders with longer timelines. Specify your preference when requesting a quote.
