# Case Study: How NW Custom Apparel Drives Cause Marketing with Emergency Web Stores

Northwest Custom Apparel believes in giving back to the community, and there's no better way to demonstrate that commitment than through **Cause Marketing**. Cause Marketing involves a business and the community working together to quickly raise money or awareness for a critical charitable cause. Every community faces moments when a person or family needs immediate assistance due to a major crisis, such as an accident, a tragic loss, or unforeseen medical problems.

This case study details two examples where Northwest Custom Apparel (NWCA) leveraged its **[in-house production speed](https://nwcustomapparel.net/24-hour-dtg-production-process/)** and **online web store technology** to mobilize community support and generate substantial funds almost overnight, demonstrating our core capability for **emergency merchandise production**.

## Case Study 1: Lakewood Police Memorial - Raising $31,000 via High-Volume Emergency Production

The tragic incident occurred in November 2009 when four Lakewood police officers were ambushed and gunned down in a Forza Coffee Shop. The crisis demanded immediate community action, and Northwest Custom Apparel stepped forward to help the families of the fallen officers.

![Lakewood Police Memorial Cap](https://nwcustomapparel.net/wp-content/uploads/2016/05/memorial-for-the-fallen-four.webp)

taken from Policemag

### The Challenge: Rapidly Mobilizing Emotional Support

The need for funds was immediate, and the demand from the Puget Sound region for a tangible symbol of support was overwhelming. A traditional order-taking process would have been too slow and complicated. NW Custom Apparel needed to produce a high-demand item quickly and handle massive walk-in sales volume.

### The Solution: The Lakewood Police Memorial Cap

Northwest Custom Apparel rapidly designed and began producing the **Lakewood Police Memorial Cap**. The decision was made to sell the cap for **$20.00** and donate **all the proceeds** directly to the officers' families.

- **Speed and Scale:** We mobilized our in-house embroidery team to prioritize the caps, ensuring a continuous supply could meet the public surge.
- **Community Mobilization:** The memorial cap became a huge success. We saw citizens, officers, and firemen lined up outside our door to buy them. The community's response was phenomenal.
- **Entity Collaboration:** The local media was instrumental; the *Ron and Don Show* picked up on the caps and broadcast the story across the airwaves. (**[verifiable news reports of the cap fundraiser](https://www.policemag.com/photo-galleries/lakewood-police-memorial)**). This led to collaboration with other trusted external entities, including Ivar’s, which donated all its profits for a day to the families.

### Proprietary Data: The Verifiable Impact

The success of the cap fundraiser demonstrated NWCA's unique ability to handle **emergency, high-volume apparel production** under intense scrutiny. At the end of the effort, Northwest Custom Apparel successfully raised **$31,000 in cap sales** for the families. This verifiable data point confirms the speed, reliability, and community trust placed in our operation.

## Case Study 2: Heroes of Twisp Wildfire Relief - Setting Up a $6,800 Online Fundraising Web Store

The summer of 2015 brought devastating wildland fires east of the Cascades, near Leavenworth and Twisp. The crisis resulted in the tragic loss of three firefighters, with one survivor, Daniel Lyons; who was also a reserve officer for the Milton Police Department, being severely burned.

### The Challenge: Generating Funds for a Specific Individual

The goal here was more personal: raise money specifically for the long-term medical care of Daniel Lyons. This required a highly efficient, traceable, and geographically flexible system. Traditional in-person sales were not practical for a statewide appeal.

### The Solution: Immediate Online Web Store Deployment

To rapidly address the specific need for the severely burned local police officer, NWCA leveraged our proprietary e-commerce system to create an online fundraising solution.

- **Service Highlight:** We created a **Heroes of Twisp Shirt** and immediately launched a dedicated, branded **[online web store](https://nwcustomapparel.net/services/webstores/)** where supporters could purchase the shirt for **$30.00**.
- **Efficiency:** The online store streamlined the entire process with handling orders, payments, fulfillment, and fund tracking with minimal overhead. This rapid deployment capability is a core service we offer all B2B clients.

### Proprietary Data: The Measurable Result

The online store proved to be the perfect mechanism for a widespread relief effort. Utilizing the platform, Northwest Custom Apparel successfully sold hundreds of shirts, raising a total of **$6,800.00**. The proceeds went directly to Daniel Lyons for his recovery and medical expenses.
