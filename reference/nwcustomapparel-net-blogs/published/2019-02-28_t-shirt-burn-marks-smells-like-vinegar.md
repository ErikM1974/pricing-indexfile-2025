# Why Does My T-Shirt Have Burn Marks and Smell Like Vinegar?

If you’ve recently bought a **DTG printed T-shirt** and noticed an unusual **vinegar smell** or what looks like **burn marks** on the fabric, you’re not alone. These occurrences are not defects but are part of the advanced DTG printing process used to create high-quality, vibrant designs. Understanding why this happens can help you appreciate the technology behind your [favorite custom T-shirt](https://nwcustomapparel.net/services/custom-t-shirt-printing/).

## The Role of Pretreatment in DTG Behind The Smell

**[Direct-to-garment printing](https://nwcustomapparel.net/direct-to-garment/)**, is a modern technology that uses advanced inkjet printing machines to apply designs directly onto fabric. Unlike traditional screen printing, which uses layers of thick inks, DTG employs water-based inks that penetrate the fibers of the garment, resulting in soft, breathable prints.

Before printing, a pretreatment solution is applied to the fabric. This is a crucial step, as the pretreatment allows the ink to sit on top of the fabric instead of soaking in. The result? Crisp, vibrant colors that don’t fade easily. This **pretreatment process is the main reason** your shirt may have a vinegar-like odor or temporary marks.

## Why Does My T-Shirt Smell Like Vinegar?

### The Purpose of the Vinegar Smell

The **vinegar smell** comes from the pretreatment liquid, which contains a fixation agent designed to bond the ink to the fabric. This is especially true for garments printed with [**Kornit DTG printers**, which use **eco-friendly, water-based inks**](https://nwcustomapparel.net/sustainability-with-dtg-printing/). The fixation process ensures that the design remains sharp and durable after washing.

### Is the Smell Harmful?

No, the **vinegar smell** is not harmful. It’s temporary and will wash out completely after the first wash. This harmless odor is a small trade-off for the environmentally friendly and sustainable printing process that DTG offers.

## What Are These Burn Marks on My T-Shirt?

### Misunderstanding Pretreatment Marks

![Pre-treat stain on a T-shirt](https://nwcustomapparel.net/wp-content/uploads/2019/02/32F14650-867D-4C2D-8E5F-205E36FDEADB-300x273.webp)

The so-called burn marks are not actual burns but pretreatment stains. These marks appear due to the interaction between the pretreatment solution and certain fabric dyes. Light-colored shirts, such as gold, red, or pastel shades, are more likely to show these marks compared to darker colors like black, navy, or gray.

### Why Do Some Shirts Have More Marks?

Fabric composition, color, and even the country of origin can affect how the pretreatment interacts with the material. For instance, a red T-shirt produced in one country might show pretreatment marks, while another red shirt from a different factory might not. This variation is due to the different chemicals and dye formulations used by manufacturers.

### How to Remove Pretreatment Marks

The good news is that these marks are temporary. Washing the shirt once in cold water with a mild detergent will remove any pretreatment residues, leaving your garment looking and smelling fresh.

## Common Questions About Vinegar Smell and Pretreatment Marks

**Will My T-Shirt Always Smell Like Vinegar?**

No, the **vinegar-like smell** is temporary. Washing the shirt once will completely eliminate the odor.

**Do All DTG T-Shirts Have Pretreatment Marks?**

Not all garments show pretreatment marks. Factors like fabric color, dye composition, and production techniques play a role. However, even when marks appear, they are temporary and will wash out.

**Are DTG-Printed Shirts Safe to Wear?**

Yes, DTG-printed shirts are completely safe to wear. The inks used are non-toxic and meet global safety standards.

**Are there certain fabrics or shirt types that are more prone to pretreatment marks?**

Yes, shirts with lighter colors, such as gold, red, and pastel shades, are more likely to show pretreatment marks. Additionally, garments made from certain fabric blends may react differently to the pretreatment solution due to variations in dye composition and texture.

**Can the vinegar smell linger if I don't wash my shirt properly?**

If the shirt isn’t washed thoroughly, a faint vinegar scent might linger. To completely eliminate the smell, use a mild detergent and ensure the shirt is rinsed well. Air drying can also help freshen up the fabric.

## How to Care for Your DTG-Printed T-Shirt

1. **First Wash**: Always wash your new T-shirt before wearing to remove the pretreatment solution.
2. **Use Cold Water**: Wash in cold water with a mild detergent to protect the print and fabric.
3. **Avoid Harsh Detergents**: Stay away from bleach or strong chemicals that might damage the print.
4. **Air Dry**: For best results, air dry your shirt instead of using a dryer.
