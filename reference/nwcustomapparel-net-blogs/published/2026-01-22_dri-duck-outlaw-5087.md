## Understanding Boulder Cloth: The Method Behind a Broken-In Heavyweight Canvas

![Close-up comparison of soft quarry-washed Boulder Cloth canvas and rigid standard canvas fabric.](https://nwcustomapparel.net/wp-content/uploads/2018/11/boulder-cloth-canvas-texture-comparison.webp)

The quarry-washing process breaks down rigid fibers for a broken-in feel without losing shell strength.

The primary material in the Outlaw 5087 is 12 oz cotton Boulder Cloth canvas. This fabric provides high tensile strength to resist tears and abrasions on job sites. Unlike standard duck canvas which feels stiff and restrictive, Boulder Cloth undergoes a quarry-washing process. This mechanical washing technique breaks down the rigid fibers of the heavy cotton weave. The result is a jacket that moves with your body rather than resisting it.

The 12 oz. weight meets rigorous **[industrial standards for breaking strength and elongation](https://store.astm.org/d5034-21.html)**, ensuring the canvas does not tear under the physical stress of manual labor.

Durability stays intact despite the softer feel. The 12 oz weight provides enough density to block wind and resist the light mist common in Seattle and Tacoma. Triple-needle stitching reinforces the high-stress seams at the shoulders and armholes. This construction prevents the jacket from pulling apart during heavy lifting or overhead work.

When you choose this material for a fleet of workers, you eliminate the common complaint that new uniforms feel like cardboard. This instant flexibility leads to higher employee satisfaction and immediate productivity.

## Design for Motion: The Technical Benefits of the Bi-Swing Back and Articulated Elbows

Physical labor requires clothing that accommodates a full range of motion. The Outlaw 5087 utilizes a bi-swing back design to solve the problem of restricted movement across the shoulders. This feature consists of two gusseted pleats located behind the armholes. When a worker reaches forward to operate machinery or climb a ladder, these pleats expand. This extra fabric prevents the jacket from tightening across the chest or pulling at the waist.

![Technical diagram of a work jacket showing bi-swing back pleats and curved articulated elbows for movement.](https://nwcustomapparel.net/wp-content/uploads/2018/11/dri-duck-5087-motion-design.webp)

Specialized seams and pleats prevent the jacket from binding or pulling during physical labor. Design for Action.

The articulated elbows provide a similar advantage. Most work jackets use a straight sleeve design that bunches at the inner elbow when the arm bends. Dri Duck inserts curved seams at the elbow to mimic the natural bend of the human arm. This prevents the sleeves from riding up the forearm when working with tools. By reducing the tension on the fabric during movement, the jacket also lasts longer. Seams are less likely to burst because the design accounts for the kinetic needs of the wearer. For business owners, this means fewer replacements and a more professional appearance for the crew throughout the workday.

## Is the Outlaw 5087 Warm Enough for a Washington Winter?

Thermal regulation in a work environment is about finding a balance between insulation and breathability. The Outlaw 5087 features a 3 oz poly-fill insulation paired with a quilted nylon lining. This combination provides sufficient warmth for typical winter temperatures in the Pacific Northwest without causing the wearer to overheat during active labor. The nylon lining is a deliberate choice. It allows the jacket to slide easily over hooded sweatshirts or flannel mid-layers, preventing the "binding" effect that occurs with fleece-lined jackets.

![Interior view of a work jacket showing quilted lining and hidden embroidery access panel.](https://nwcustomapparel.net/wp-content/uploads/2018/11/dri-duck-outlaw-interior-features.webp)

The internal construction hides embroidery backing and ensures the insulation remains undisturbed. Smart Interior Design.

The jacket includes ribbed gator sleeve cuffs and a corduroy collar to trap heat. These cuffs sit inside the sleeve, creating a seal around the wrist that prevents cold air from entering. The corduroy collar provides a soft surface against the neck and stands up to block wind when needed. While the jacket is not a substitute for a dedicated waterproof raincoat during a heavy downpour, the dense weave of the Boulder Cloth sheds light moisture. This makes it the ideal outer layer for workers who move frequently between outdoor sites and indoor shops.

## FAQs About the Dri Duck 5087

**Does the Dri Duck Outlaw 5087 run true to size?**

The Outlaw is cut with a "relaxed work fit." It runs slightly larger than a standard fashion jacket to allow for layering over heavy sweatshirts or thermal base layers. If your employees intend to wear only a t-shirt underneath, you should order one size smaller than their usual measurement.

**Will the Boulder Cloth canvas shrink in the wash?**

Dri Duck pre-washes the Boulder Cloth to minimize shrinkage. However, the 100% cotton shell can still shrink slightly if dried on high heat. We recommend washing in cold water and tumble drying on low or hang drying to maintain the original fit and the integrity of the nylon lining.

**Is the 5087 Outlaw jacket waterproof?**

No. It is water-resistant. The dense 12 oz weave sheds light rain and mist, but it will eventually soak through in a heavy downpour. For crews working in constant rain, we recommend treating the canvas with a specialized wax or DWR spray, though this can change the breathability of the fabric.

**Can you put a full-back logo on this jacket?**

Yes. The 5087 has a large, clear back panel without horizontal seams. This makes it an ideal canvas for large-scale company branding, such as landscaping or construction logos that need to be visible from a distance.
