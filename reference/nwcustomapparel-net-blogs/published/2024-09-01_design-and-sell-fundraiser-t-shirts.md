# How Washington Groups Design and Sell Fundraiser T-Shirts

## Why T-Shirt Fundraisers Work

People donate more when they receive something tangible. A $25 t-shirt purchase feels like a transaction, not a handout. The supporter gets a product. The cause gets revenue.

At Northwest Custom Apparel in Milton, Washington, we have produced fundraiser t-shirts for schools, non-profits, and community groups since 1977. We have seen campaigns clear $2,000 on a single 200-shirt order. We have also seen campaigns lose money because the organizer chose the wrong production method or priced the shirt too low.

This article covers what we have learned from the production floor. Not marketing theory. Practical guidance on how to design, price, and sell fundraiser t-shirts that actually generate profit for your cause.

## Designing a Fundraiser Shirt That Sells

### The Design Must Work From Across a Room

Fundraiser shirts are impulse purchases. Someone sees another person wearing the shirt at the grocery store or on social media and decides to buy one. The design must be readable at a distance.

We recommend one central image, one short phrase, and no more than three colors for screen-printed orders. A [breast cancer awareness shirt with a pink ribbon](https://nwcustomapparel.net/breast-cancer-awareness/) and "Walk for a Cure" sells. A shirt with six logos, three slogans, and a QR code does not.

![fundrasing](https://nwcustomapparel.net/wp-content/uploads/2017/05/fundrasing-with-t-shirts-300x105-1.webp)

For photo-based designs (a memorial portrait, a school group photo), [direct-to-garment printing](https://nwcustomapparel.net/direct-to-garment/) handles full-color images without the color limit. These work best for small pre-order campaigns where you are testing demand before a bulk run.

### Align the Design With the Cause

A school fundraiser should feature the mascot or school colors. A memorial fundraiser should use the honoree's photo or a meaningful symbol. A charity run should include the event name and date.

The design is not just decoration. It is the reason someone buys the shirt instead of donating directly. Make it specific enough that the shirt cannot be mistaken for generic merchandise.

### Why Pre-Orders Reduce Risk

The biggest risk in a t-shirt fundraiser is ordering 200 shirts and selling 80. You are left with inventory, sunk costs, and no profit.

Our [custom webstores](https://nwcustomapparel.net/services/webstores/) solve this. We build a branded storefront for your cause. Supporters select their size, pay with a credit card, and we produce and ship directly. You collect the profit margin without touching inventory or handling payments.

Pre-order campaigns work best for:

- School groups with uncertain participation
- First-time fundraisers with no sales history
- Causes with supporters spread across multiple cities or states

For established annual events (a yearly charity run, a recurring school spirit week), bulk screen printing is more profitable because the per-unit cost is lower. You need confidence in your sales volume.

## NW Custom Apparel's Production Methods for Fundraiser Orders

### Screen Printing for Bulk Campaigns

For orders of 100 pieces or more, [screen printing](https://nwcustomapparel.net/services/screen-printing/) is the most cost-effective method. The setup cost is spread across the full order. The print is durable through repeated washing. The colors are vivid and opaque on dark garments.

Turnaround is 10 business days. Rush production is available for 5-day turnaround when our schedule allows. We recommend ordering at least 3 weeks before your event date to allow for proofing, production, and delivery.

### DTG Printing for Pre-Order and Small Campaigns

For campaigns under 24 pieces or for testing a design before a bulk order, [direct-to-garment printing](https://nwcustomapparel.net/direct-to-garment/) is the better choice. No screen setup. No minimum order. Full-color photo printing. 48-hour turnaround.

We often recommend DTG for memorial fundraisers where the design includes a portrait. The photo quality is higher than screen print. The emotional value of the image justifies the higher per-unit cost.

### Embroidery for Premium Fundraisers

Some causes benefit from a premium product. A [custom embroidered](https://nwcustomapparel.net/services/custom-embroidery/) polo or jacket sells at a higher price point and generates more profit per unit. We have [produced embroidered caps for a Tacoma fallen officer memorial fundraiser](https://nwcustomapparel.net/cause-marketing-custom-apparel-case-study/). The jackets sold at $20 each. The production cost was $10. The memorial fund cleared $10 per jacket. At the end, we raised $31,000 in cap sales for the families.

Embroidery works best for:

- Causes with older, more affluent supporters
- Memorial campaigns where longevity matters
- Annual events where the garment becomes a tradition

## Promoting Your Fundraiser

### Social Media With Real Photos

Stock photos of the shirt design generate less engagement than photos of real people wearing the shirt. Take a photo of the first sample shirt on an actual person. Post it. Ask your board members, volunteers, or early buyers to post photos wearing the shirt and tag your cause.

![fundraiser-t-shirt](https://nwcustomapparel.net/wp-content/uploads/2017/05/fundraiser-t-shirt-300x300-1.webp)

### Event Distribution

If your fundraiser is tied to an event (a 5K, a school play, a community festival), distribute shirts at the event. People buy shirts on impulse when they see others wearing them. The shirt becomes part of the experience, not just a product.

For event-based fundraisers, we deliver sorted by size to your event location. No one hunts through boxes. Your volunteers hand out shirts and collect payments efficiently.

### Partner With Local Businesses

A Tacoma coffee shop displayed fundraiser shirts on their counter and collected orders for a local youth sports league. The shop took no commission. They supported the cause and drove foot traffic. The league sold 40 additional shirts through the partnership.

Local businesses are often willing to display flyers or sell shirts for community causes. Ask. The worst outcome is a polite no.

## What Successful Fundraisers Do Differently

**They set a specific goal.** "We need to raise $3,000 for the band trip to Chicago" is more compelling than "support our school." People donate more when they know exactly what their money buys.

**They limit the sales window.** A 2-week pre-order campaign creates urgency. An open-ended campaign drags on and loses momentum.

**They offer multiple sizes and styles.** Youth sizes, adult unisex, and women's fitted cuts maximize your potential buyer pool. We stock blanks in XS through 5XL across all major brands.

**They follow up with results.** After the fundraiser closes, tell supporters how much was raised and what it will fund. Transparency builds trust for next year's campaign.

## FAQs

**How do I choose the right t-shirt for my fundraiser?**

Choosing the right t-shirt involves balancing cost and quality. Look for soft, durable fabrics that people will want to wear often. Select colors and sizes that cater to a wide audience to maximize appeal.

**How can I market my fundraiser t-shirts effectively?**

Use multiple channels to promote your campaign. Post frequently on social media, create an email newsletter, and reach out to local influencers who can help spread the word. Also, encourage supporters to share photos wearing their shirts

**How much profit can I expect from a t-shirt fundraiser?**

Profits depend on your pricing and production costs. Aim for a reasonable markup per shirt, typically between $5-$10, while keeping prices affordable. Selling in higher volumes can significantly boost your total revenue.

**How do I design a shirt that resonates with my audience?**

Focus on [creating a design](https://nwcustomapparel.net/how-to-design-a-custom-t-shirt/) that aligns with your cause. Use bold, simple graphics and meaningful messages. Involve your community by asking for feedback or holding a design contest to ensure your shirts resonate with potential buyers.
