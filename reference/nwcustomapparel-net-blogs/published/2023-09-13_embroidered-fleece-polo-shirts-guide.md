## Why Choose Custom Embroidery for Your Apparel?

While many decoration methods exist, embroidery remains the gold standard for branded apparel. It offers a level of quality and durability that other techniques simply can't match.

### Durability & Quality

Unlike screen printing or heat transfers, which can fade or crack over time, a professionally embroidered logo is virtually indestructible. Our use of high-quality **polyester threads** ensures that your design will remain vibrant, no matter how many times it's washed. The stitching is a permanent part of the garment, designed to withstand wear, tear, and frequent use. This longevity makes embroidery a smart long-term investment.

### Professional & Premium Look

There's a reason why corporate uniforms and premium brands prefer embroidery. The rich texture and three-dimensional feel of a stitched logo instantly elevates the garment, giving it a high-end, polished appearance. Whether it's a subtle logo on the chest or a prominent emblem, embroidery sends a clear message of quality and professionalism.

## How to Choose the Right Garment for Embroidery

Selecting the perfect garment for your embroidery project is key to a successful outcome.

### Fleece vs. Polo: When to Use Each

The choice between fleece and a polo shirt often comes down to your needs. **Fleecewear** is ideal for cooler climates, outdoor events, or casual work environments that prioritize comfort and warmth. **Polo shirts** are a more versatile option, perfect for corporate settings, trade shows, retail staff, or any situation where a polished, professional look is required.

### Fabric Matters

The [fabric's composition and texture](https://nwcustomapparel.net/polo-shirt-material-importance/) have a big impact on the final embroidered result. **Pique knit**, with its waffle-like texture, holds stitches very well, making it a favorite for embroidered polo shirts. **Jersey knit** is a softer, smoother fabric that can also be embroidered, but requires a skilled hand to prevent puckering. For fleece, the embroidery must be carefully **digitized** to prevent the stitches from sinking into the soft pile.

## FAQs

**What kind of shirts are best for embroidery?**

Polo shirts and button-down shirts made from pique, twill, or interlock knits are excellent for embroidery due to their tight weave and structured fabric.

**What fabrics are not good for embroidery?**

Thin, stretchy fabrics like jersey knits without a backing and very lightweight materials are generally not ideal, as they can pucker and distort the design. Silks and very loose weaves are also difficult to embroider.

**What is the best thread for embroidery?**

Polyester thread is the industry standard. It is strong, durable, colorfast, and resists fading from repeated washing and exposure to UV light.

**Can you embroider on fleece?**

Yes, we can. Fleece is a popular choice for embroidery. A firm backing is used to stabilize the fabric, ensuring the stitches sit cleanly on top of the soft pile without getting lost.
