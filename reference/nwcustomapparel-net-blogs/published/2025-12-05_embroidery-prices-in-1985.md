# The Evolution of Custom Apparel: 1985 vs. Today

This article revisits the pioneering days of **Northwest Custom Apparel** (formerly Northwest Embroidery) to show how far the industry has come. **Jim Mickelson**, our founder, reflects on our 48 years in business, providing authentic insights into how technology and market needs have evolved. This history establishes our experience and authority in the custom embroidery and apparel space.

## 1985: The Start of a Legacy

In 1985, **Northwest Custom Apparel** installed the **[first cap embroidery machines](https://nwcustomapparel.net/first-embroidery-machine-arrives-washington/) in the Pacific Northwest**. That technology required intense manual labor and ingenuity:

| **1985 Operational Reality** | **Modern Comparison** |
| --- | --- |
| **Digitizing:** We called it **"[punching](https://nwcustomapparel.net/logo-digitizing-for-embroidery/)"** the designs. Operators devised long **jump stitches** to minimize thread jumps, which were then trimmed by hand. | Today, **Digitized Embroidery** software automatically optimizes designs and thread paths, creating precise, high-density files with minimal jumps. |
| **Trimming:** There were no automatic trimmers. Operators had to trim all caps using small **embroidery scissors** by hand. | Modern **high-speed embroidery machines** use automated trimming systems, ensuring clean finishes and consistency at production speed. |
| **Communication:** Business relied on telexes. Purchasing a **fax machine** was a major competitive advantage, allowing us to transmit an 8x11 sheet of information to **Taiwan** for emblem orders. | Now, orders are processed and coordinated globally using **high-speed internet** and cloud-based systems. Turnaround happens in days, not weeks. |

## The Economic Shift: Prices and Value

![cap ad](https://nwcustomapparel.net/wp-content/uploads/2022/11/cap-ad.webp)

Newspaper Advertisement of Embroidered Polo Shirts and Caps from 1985

Comparing pricing from 1985 to today describes the huge changes in labor costs, material sourcing, and production efficiency. The old prices serve as a benchmark for the value we deliver now.

- **Polo Shirts:** A good quality polo shirt sold for **$7.50 each, including embroidery**. The blank shirt cost us **$4.75**.
- **Caps:** We sold embroidered caps for **$4.00 each**. A screen-printed cap cost the customer about **$1.25** retail. The same cap sells for **over $20.00** today.
- **Labor Cost:** With the minimum wage at **$3.35 an hour**, an operator producing 24 pieces per hour meant our cost for embroidery was only **14 cents** per piece. We could import caps for **95 cents** each.

The low costs of 1985 let us compete directly with Asian manufacturers. This efficiency was possible then through incredibly low labor costs and basic technology. Today, modern embroidery machinery and advanced logistics allow us to adapt and change in a dynamic environment, prioritizing quality and speed over minimal price points.
