# Custom Hoodies for Heavy Equipment and Construction Companies: Choosing the Right Print Method

Heavy equipment companies, diesel shops, construction crews, and excavation businesses order custom hoodies for two different reasons, and those reasons require two different production methods.

The first reason: crew gear. Logo on the front, company name on the back, ordered 24–100 pieces at a time for the field crew. This is a screen printing job.

The second reason: branded merchandise or collector apparel. Photo-realistic image of a Caterpillar dozer, a Komatsu excavator, or a customer's specific machine such as full-color photographic detail on a dark hoodie, ordered in small quantities for drivers, collectors, or a company merchandise table. This is a DTG job.

Getting the method wrong costs money. This article explains the difference so you know which to request before you call.

## When DTG Printing Is the Right Choice for Heavy Equipment Hoodies

### What makes DTG the right method for photo-realistic machine apparel?

DTG printing uses industrial inkjet technology to spray water-based CMYK inks directly onto the fabric, layer by layer. It reads your design from a digital file and prints it without screens, without setup fees, and without minimum order requirements.

For heavy equipment and diesel industry apparel, DTG is the right method when:

**Your design is a photograph or photo-realistic image of a machine.** Screen printing separates artwork into individual solid ink colors, typically 1–6 colors, and burns each onto a separate screen. A photographic image of a Caterpillar D9 bulldozer with weathered paint, reflective glass, and dimensional shadows cannot be reproduced accurately through screen printing without enormous color counts and cost. DTG prints the full photographic image in one pass, reproducing every detail the original photo captured.

**Your order is under 24 pieces.** Screen printing setup costs run $25–$45 per screen per color. A 6-color design means $150–$270 in setup before the first hoodie is printed. Across 6 hoodies, that adds $25–$45 per piece just for setup. DTG has no setup fee, the cost per piece is the same whether you order 1 or 20.

**Every hoodie needs a different image.** Screen printing runs the same design on every garment in a run. If a diesel shop wants 8 hoodies, each featuring a different customer's machine, DTG prints each one individually from its own file with no additional cost per variation.

NW Custom Apparel runs DTG production on Kornit Storm industrial printers at our Milton, WA facility. The Kornit Storm is an industrial-grade machine built for commercial production runs, not a consumer-grade printer. It applies a white underbase to dark garments first, then lays CMYK color layers over it, which is what makes photographic images vibrant on black and dark navy hoodies.

The process:

**File review.** Our graphics team assessed the photo for resolution and print compatibility. High-resolution photos (minimum 300 DPI at print size) reproduce cleanly. Low-resolution phone photos taken from a distance require cleanup before they print well.

**Design preparation.** The team cropped the truck image, removed the background, and added Heartland Transport's company name and a brief tagline below the machine image. Total design time: under one hour. (Our standard includes 1 hour of design time. Additional work is billed at $60 per hour.)

**Pre-production proof.** Heartland Transport reviewed a digital proof showing the design on a black hoodie mockup before we printed anything.

**Production on the Kornit Storm.** The hoodie was loaded onto the printer platen, the machine applied the white underbase to the print area, and the CMYK layers followed. Total print time per piece: approximately 4–7 minutes.

**Result.** A photographic-quality print that shows the truck in full detail like paint texture, reflective surfaces, dimensional depth, on a black hoodie. The kind of result that screen printing cannot produce at any price point for a design with that level of color complexity.

## When Screen Printing Is the Right Choice for Heavy Equipment Crew Hoodies

### What makes screen printing better for construction crew gear?

Most Washington construction companies, excavation businesses, and heavy equipment operators need crew hoodies with a company logo, not a photographic machine image. This is a fundamentally different design: a solid logo in 1–4 colors, often with a company name, maybe a tagline or location.

For this type of order, screen printing is the better method when:

**Your order is 24 pieces or more.** Once setup cost is spread across 24+ pieces, screen printing's per-piece cost drops significantly below DTG. At 48 pieces, a 2-color screen printed logo hoodie costs substantially less per unit than the same hoodie printed via DTG. At 72 or more pieces, the gap is larger.

**Your design is a logo or wordmark, not a photograph.** Screen printing lays thick, opaque plastisol ink directly on fleece, which is why white logos on black hoodies look bold and solid. Plastisol also holds through job-site washing better than water-based DTG ink over repeated weekly wash cycles.

**You need safety striping on the hoodie.** For construction crew hoodies that need reflective safety stripes on the sleeves or back, screen printing with plastisol safety ink or reflective ink is the standard. DTG cannot produce safety stripe effects.

**Your crew cycles through hoodies regularly.** Washington construction companies on 12–18 month crew replacement cycles reorder the same hoodie design repeatedly. Screen printing stores the artwork after the first run, and reorders use the same screens at lower per-piece cost than the initial setup run.

Use this to decide before you call:

| Crew hoodies with company logo | Logo, 1–4 colors | Under 24 pieces | DTG |
| Machine photo apparel | Photographic image | Any quantity | DTG |
| Mixed crew: same logo, different sizes | Logo, 1–4 colors | 24+ pieces total | Screen printing |
| Collector/merchandise hoodies | Photographic or full-color | Small batch (1–20) | DTG |

**What is the minimum order for screen printed hoodies?**

Screen printed hoodie orders start at 24 pieces per design. For orders under 24 pieces with a logo-based design, DTG is the better option.

**Can I get different machines on each hoodie in the same order?**

Yes. Because DTG prints each piece from its own digital file, every hoodie in your order can feature a different machine image with no additional setup cost per variation.

**What resolution does my photo need to be for DTG printing?**

300 DPI at the intended print size is the minimum for a clean photographic result. High-resolution photos from a digital camera or recent smartphone work well. Low-resolution images downloaded from websites often require cleanup before they print at acceptable quality.

**Can I get both a front photo and a back logo on the same hoodie?**

Yes. A back logo print is available as an add-on to the front machine image. Mention this in your quote request and we will confirm pricing.

**Do you print on dark hoodies?**

Yes. The Kornit Storm applies a white underbase to dark garments before printing the color layers. This is what makes photographic images vibrant on black, charcoal, and dark navy hoodies.

**What size hoodies do you carry for construction crews?**

We carry sizes Small through 5XL. There is a small upcharge for 2XL and above on most blank styles, which is standard across the industry. Tell us your size breakdown when requesting a quote and we will confirm availability.
