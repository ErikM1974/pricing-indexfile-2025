## The Necessity of Water-Based Inks

Digital printing technology made a huge shift away from traditional, solvent-based screen-printing inks. **Plastisol**, a common screen-printing ink, contains PVC (polyvinyl chloride) and requires plasticizers to keep it liquid. Our industrial Direct-to-Garment process exclusively uses Water-Based Pigment Inks.

This pivot to water-based chemistry means the ink is free of **Heavy Toxic Materials** and **Heavy Metals** common in older printing methods. To understand the complete production advantages of this technology, read our guide on **[Direct-to-Garment printing processes](https://nwcustomapparel.net/sustainability-with-dtg-printing/).**

## The Proof: Oeko-Tex Certification

The definitive evidence for ink safety comes from third-party verification. We cite the **Oeko-Tex** standard, the world's most recognized label for textiles tested for harmful substances.

### Oeko-Tex Eco-Passport: Certifying the Chemical Product

The Oeko-Tex Eco-Passport certifies the chemical products themselves, such as the ink, pre-treatment, and other textile auxiliaries. Our [Kornit **NeoPigment™ Eco-Rapid Ink** holds th](https://knowledge-hub.circle-economy.com/article/9205)[i](https://knowledge-hub.circle-economy.com/article/9205)[s certification](https://knowledge-hub.circle-economy.com/article/9205), which verify its components are safe for human and environmental health and comply with the **ZDHC MRSL** (Zero Discharge of Hazardous Chemicals Manufacturing Restricted Substance List). This ensures the raw materials of the ink do not contain banned substances.

The DTG inks meet all government standards for inks used on children's apparel, guaranteeing a final product with vibrant color that will outlast the life of the garment.

### Oeko-Tex Standard 100 - Product Class I: The Infant Standard

The Oeko-Tex Standard 100 tests the final textile product. The requirements are categorized into four **Product Classes** based on consumer sensitivity:

- **Product Class I:** This is the most stringent level, mandatory for **Textiles for Babies and Small Children up to 3 Years**.
- **Product Class II:** For items with direct, extensive skin contact (e.g., underwear).
- **Product Classes III & IV:** For materials with no skin contact or home textiles.

For a detailed understanding of the most stringent textile safety requirements, consult the **[Oeko-Tex STANDARD 100 Product Classes documentation](https://www.oeko-tex.com/fileadmin/user_upload/Marketing_Materialien/STANDARD_100/Factsheet/STANDARD_100/OEKO-TEX_STANDARD_100_Factsheet_EN.pdf)**. By relying on inks proven to meet the highest limits of **Product Class I**, we specifically validate the claim that our prints are safe for infants, guaranteeing the highest level of human-ecological protection for your apparel.

## Chemical Exclusions: Meeting Regulatory Substances

The current standard of safety requires the exclusion of specific harmful chemical substances. The Oeko-Tex certification guarantees our inks are free of these substances:

- **Formaldehyde-Free:** Formaldehyde is a known irritant and is strictly limited in all Oeko-Tex standards, especially in Product Class I materials.
- **APEO-Free:** The inks are free of **Alkylphenol Ethoxylates** (APEOs), which are regulated due to their harmful effect on aquatic life.
- **Heavy Metals:** The inks are tested against limits for heavy metals, including lead and cadmium.

The ability of our **Kornit** systems to print without pre-treatment on many fabrics, and with specialized eco-friendly fixation agents when necessary, further reduces the overall chemical load and produces zero wastewater, a sustainable practice central to our operation.
