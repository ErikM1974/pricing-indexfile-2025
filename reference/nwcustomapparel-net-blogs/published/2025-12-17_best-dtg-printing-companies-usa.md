## What is DTG Printing on Shirts?

![best t-shirts with dtg printing](https://nwcustomapparel.net/wp-content/uploads/2023/11/best-shirts-for-dtg-printing-300x225.webp)

DTG printing acts like an inkjet printer for clothing. The machine sprays water-based pigment inks directly into the fabric fibers. [This method differs from screen printing](https://nwcustomapparel.net/dtg-vs-dtf-vs-screen-printing/) since it requires no physical screens or messy setups. It excels at reproducing photographs, complex gradients, and intricate fine lines. Modern industrial systems now rival the speed of traditional methods for small to medium runs.

### The Rise of Industrial Quality

Early digital printers suffered from slow speeds and fragile prints. Today, industrial machines change the equation. Systems like the Kornit Atlas Max produce retail-ready garments with a soft hand feel. These machines include automated steps that remove human error. This shift means businesses can now expect high durability and vibrant colors from digital prints.

## Why Your Choice of DTG Company Matters

The quality gap between providers is wide. Some shops use converted desktop printers that lack power and consistency. Others use massive industrial systems that ensure every shirt looks the same.

### Aggregators vs. Direct Manufacturers

Online platforms like Printful or Printify act as middlemen. They send your order to a network of different print shops. This leads to inconsistent quality because you never know which machine prints your shirt. Direct manufacturers like Northwest Custom Apparel own their equipment. They control the entire process from artwork prep to final curing.

### Is DTG Printing Good Quality?

Quality depends on the equipment and the operator's skill. Industrial-grade prints feel soft and stay vibrant after fifty washes. Low-end prints might feel like a thick sticker or crack after one wash. Choosing a company with top-tier technology ensures your customers receive a product they actually want to wear.

## DTG Printed Shirt Quality: The Technical Factors

Achieving a perfect print requires more than just a good machine. The chemistry and physics of the process matter.

### The Substrate Choice: 100% Cotton

Cotton fibers are the best canvas for digital inks. They absorb the water-based pigments effectively. While some advanced machines handle blends, pure cotton offers the most vibrant results. We recommend high-end blanks like the Bella+Canvas 3001 or Cotton Heritage premium tees. These fabrics provide a smooth surface that allows the ink to lay flat.

### Automated Pre-treatment

Dark garments require a white ink base. For the white ink to stick, the shirt needs a pre-treatment solution. Industrial machines apply this fluid automatically during the print cycle. This ensures an even coat. Manual spraying often leads to "box marks" or uneven colors. Northwest Custom Apparel's automated system eliminates these issues.

### Curing for Durability

After the ink hits the shirt, it stays wet. It must go through a dryer to bond with the fibers. Proper heat and time settings are vital. If the temperature is too low, the ink will wash out. If it is too high, the fabric might scorch. Professional shops use conveyor dryers to ensure every part of the design cures perfectly.

## DTG Printing No Minimum and Scalability

One of the biggest advantages of digital printing is flexibility. You can print one shirt or one hundred with the same level of ease.

### Testing Your Market

New clothing brands often use DTG to test designs. They print a few shirts to see which ones sell. This avoids the risk of sitting on boxes of unsold screen-printed inventory. Once a design becomes a bestseller, they can scale up to larger runs.

### DTG vs. Screen Printing Cost

Digital printing has a flat cost per shirt. Screen printing has high setup fees but lower per-unit costs for bulk. The break-even point usually falls around 24 to 48 units. For orders under two dozen, DTG is almost always the more affordable path.

## FAQs

**Who is the best DTG printing company?**

The best company is a direct manufacturer with industrial equipment. Northwest Custom Apparel is the leader for B2B and high-quality orders in the USA.

**Is DTG better than Screen Printing?**

It depends on the design. DTG is better for photos and small orders. [Screen printing is better for large bulk orders](https://nwcustomapparel.net/bulk-screen-printed-tees-order/) with simple colors.

**Why is my DTG print fading?**

Fading usually happens due to poor curing or low-quality ink. Professional shops use industrial dryers to prevent this.

**Can I print DTG on polyester?**

Most DTG ink is for cotton. For 100% polyester, we usually recommend [DTF (Direct to Film) printing](https://nwcustomapparel.net/services/direct-to-film-printing/).

**How many colors can I use in DTG?**

You have no limit. Since it works like an inkjet printer, you can print millions of colors in a single pass.
