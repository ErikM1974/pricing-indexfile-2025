## Richardson 112 Unit Cost Structure

Corporate embroidery pricing derives from 3 cost components: blank goods, digitizing, and stitch execution.

**Blank Goods Cost:** [Richardson 112 wholesale pricing data](https://www.capwholesalers.com/richardson-best-seller-112) shows $9.75–$14.42 blank cap costs depending on colorway complexity and order volume. Standard colorways (black, navy, charcoal) occupy the lower end. Custom colorways and licensed patterns occupy the higher end. Northwest Custom Apparel maintains inventory of 12 standard colorways at the $9.75 price point for immediate production. Blank cap durability depends on **[polypropylene material specifications](https://nwcustomapparel.net/polypropylene-injection-molding-richardson-112-snapback-hats/)** covered in our technical guide.

**Digitizing Cost:** Logo conversion to stitch files costs $75.00–$100.00 one-time per design. Complex 3D puff embroidery requires $100.00–$150.00 digitizing due to foam integration and density mapping. This cost amortizes across order volume: $1.04 per cap on a 24-cap order, $0.10 per cap on a 1,000-cap order.

**Stitch Execution Cost:** Thread, machine time, and labor comprise $0.0015 per stitch. An 8,000-stitch logo costs $12.00 in execution. A 12,000-stitch 3D puff logo costs $18.00. Rush processing (3–5 day turnaround) adds 25% premium. Standard processing (7–11 day turnaround) maintains base pricing.

## Corporate Procurement Decision Framework

Procurement managers evaluate 4 factors when selecting Richardson 112 embroidery vendors.

**Factor 1: Unit Cost Validation**  
Request itemized quotes separating blank goods, digitizing, and stitch costs. Vendors bundling these components obscure true unit economics. Northwest Custom Apparel provides line-item breakdowns enabling accurate budget allocation.

**Factor 2: Capacity Verification**  
Confirm vendor machine count and daily throughput. Single-machine operations bottleneck at 64 caps per day. Northwest Custom Apparel operates two 8-head Tajima machines delivering 128-cap daily capacity with overflow scheduling.

**Factor 3: Geographic Proximity**  
Milton, Washington location provides same-day pickup for Seattle and Tacoma clients. Shipping from distant vendors adds 2–3 days transit time and $50–$150 freight costs per order.

**Factor 4: Quality Consistency**  
Request 3–5 physical samples from previous production runs. Evaluate logo registration consistency, thread tension uniformity, and snap closure function. Northwest Custom Apparel provides production samples from actual machine runs, not mockups.
