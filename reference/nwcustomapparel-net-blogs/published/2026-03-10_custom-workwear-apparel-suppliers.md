## What Makes a Custom Workwear Apparel Supplier Different from a Blank Goods Wholesaler?

The difference matters more than most buyers realize before they place their first bulk order.

![A diagram comparing the complex multi-step wholesale process with the streamlined single-source custom workwear supplier model.](https://nwcustomapparel.net/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/custom-workwear-supplier-vs-wholesaler-workflow-1024x559.webp)

Consolidating your garment sourcing and decoration into one vendor saves an average of 5–7 days in lead time.

**A blank goods wholesaler** sells undecorated garments to businesses and decorators. SanMar — founded in 1971 and headquartered in Issaquah, Washington — is the largest wholesale apparel supplier in the United States, distributing over 30 brands from nine warehouse locations nationwide. S&S Activewear and Alphabroder operate similar models. These companies sell garments but do not embroider, screen print, or otherwise decorate them.

**A custom workwear apparel supplier** sources garments AND applies decoration. This means one point of contact, one pre-production approval, one shipment, and one invoice. For businesses ordering 6–500 pieces at a time, this structure eliminates the coordination delay that occurs when a blank goods order arrives at a decorator three days late and pushes the embroidery run into the following week.

The practical differences between the two models:

|  |  |  |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Factor** | **Blank Goods Wholesaler** | **Custom Workwear Supplier** |
| **Garment sourcing** | Yes | Yes |
| **Logo decoration** | No — separate vendor required | Yes — in-house |
| **Pre-production sample** | Not applicable | Yes — before full run |
| **Single invoice** | No — garments billed separately from decoration | Yes |
| **Typical lead time** | 2–5 days blanks + 5–10 days at decorator | 10 business days total |
| **Brand selection** | Wide — all brands they stock | Curated — brands we know decorate well |

For businesses placing their first bulk workwear order, the single-vendor model reduces complexity. For businesses with existing relationships at a blank goods wholesaler who want decoration only, contract embroidery on customer-supplied garments is also available — ask before shipping blanks to confirm compatibility.

## Which Decoration Method Works for Bulk Workwear Orders?

The right method depends on fabric type, logo complexity, garment quantity, and durability requirements. Choosing the wrong method for the fabric causes logo failure — thread blowouts on embroidery, ink cracking on screen prints, or adhesion failure on DTF transfers. Here is how each method maps to workwear applications.

![A comparison table showing the best uses for embroidery, screen printing, and direct-to-film printing on workwear.](https://nwcustomapparel.net/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/workwear-decoration-method-matrix-1024x559.webp)

Use embroidery for longevity on heavy cotton, and DTF for vibrant logos on polyester performance gear.

### Embroidery on Work Jackets and Crew Uniforms - When Thread Outperforms Ink

Embroidery is the most durable decoration method for workwear. Embroidered logos survive industrial washing cycles, abrasion, and outdoor exposure without fading or peeling. This is why it's the standard for work jackets, polo shirts, caps, and beanies in professional uniform programs.

**Fabric requirement:** 12 oz. cotton duck canvas handles 15,000+ stitch count cleanly. Mid-weight twill (6–8 oz.) works reliably at under 10,000 stitches. Lightweight polyester blends (under 5 oz.) are generally not suitable for dense embroidery without specialty backing.

**Placement:** Left chest (standard for corporate logos), right chest (name/title), sleeve, back yoke. Carhartt Company Gear limits embroidery to a maximum width of 3.25 inches per location. NW Custom Apparel does not apply this restriction.

**Minimum order:** 6 pieces at NW Custom Apparel

**Turnaround:** 10 business days from pre-production sample approval

For full detail on how **[logo embroidery on bulk work jackets and crew uniforms](https://nwcustomapparel.net/services/custom-embroidery/)** works at different stitch counts and fabric weights, see our custom embroidery services page.

### Screen Printing on Bulk T-Shirts and Hi-Vis Workwear — When Volume and Scale Matter

Screen printing is the most cost-effective method for large print areas on high quantities. It delivers vibrant, long-lasting results on 100% cotton and 50/50 cotton-polyester blends. It's the required method for logo application on ANSI-certified Hi-Vis substrates where embroidery hooping would compromise the garment's reflective tape placement and potentially void the [ANSI/ISEA 107-2015 standard](https://webstore.ansi.org/standards/isea/ansiisea1072015)'s minimum retroreflective material requirements.

**Ink types:** Plastisol (standard, high durability), water-based (softer hand feel on fashion blanks), discharge (removes dye for vintage effect on 100% cotton).

**Fabric requirement:** 100% cotton or 50/50 blends for best results. High-polyester content fabrics require low-cure plastisol or specialty inks to prevent dye migration.

**Color limitation:** Each color in the design requires a separate screen. Carhartt Company Gear limits screen printing to 4 ink colors maximum. NW Custom Apparel quotes based on color count — more colors increase setup cost but not per-unit cost proportionally at scale.

**Minimum order:** 24 pieces at NW Custom Apparel for screen printing

For construction and warehouse crews needing **[custom screen printing for bulk workwear orders with ANSI-compliant Hi-Vis application](https://nwcustomapparel.net/services/screen-printing/)**, screen printing delivers the lowest per-unit cost at quantities of 24+.

### DTF Printing on Polyester-Blend Workwear — When Embroidery and Screen Printing Don't Apply

Direct-to-Film printing uses a heat-transfer process that applies full-color designs to nearly any fabric substrate — including polyester, nylon, and synthetic blends where embroidery backing would distort the garment and screen printing ink adhesion is unreliable.

DTF is the correct method for moisture-wicking polos, softshell jackets, performance workwear, and athletic-style crew shirts where the fabric content is primarily synthetic. It carries no color limitation and no separate screen cost per color, which makes it economical for small-quantity, multi-color logo runs.

**Fabric compatibility:** Cotton, polyester, nylon, blends — essentially any fabric that tolerates heat press application (above 300°F for 10–15 seconds)

**Color limitation:** None — full-color CMYK output

**Durability:** DTF transfers on properly prepared substrates survive 40–50 wash cycles before visible fade begins under normal laundering conditions. Embroidery outlasts DTF on heavy-use workwear.

**No minimum:** DTF can be applied to single pieces. Most economical at 12+ for setup efficiency.

For polyester-blend workwear where embroidery backing would cause fabric distortion, see our **[DTF printing service for synthetic workwear substrates](https://nwcustomapparel.net/services/direct-to-film-printing/)**.

A workwear supplier sells blank garments. A decorator applies logos and branding. NW Custom Apparel does both — sourcing the garment from our distributor network and applying the decoration in-house.
