# Customization & Print Methods

Welcome to the central hub for custom apparel decoration! If you’re looking to brand a t-shirt, create custom uniforms, or design promotional merchandise, you've come to the right place. Choosing the right method is the first and most critical step in bringing your vision to life.

Here at Northwest Custom Apparel, we've spent decades perfecting every major customization method. We know that a one-size-fits-all approach doesn't work. This guide is designed to help you understand the core differences between each technique—from classic **screen printing** to modern **direct-to-garment (DTG) printing**—so you can make an informed decision for your brand.

Whether you're a small business owner, a marketing manager, or a creative designer, this page is your roadmap to professional-quality branded apparel.

## **Our Customization Options**

We offer a full spectrum of decoration services, each with its own unique strengths. Click on any of the options below to dive deeper into the world of that specific method.

### **Screen Printing: The Classic Choice for Bold, Vibrant Designs**

**Description:** Screen printing is a timeless method known for its durability, bold colors, and long-lasting quality. It's the go-to choice for high-volume orders and designs that need to stand out. From custom t-shirts to hoodies and jackets, screen printing delivers a vibrant, professional result that can withstand a lifetime of washes.

### **Embroidery: Discover the Premium, Professional Look**

**Description:** When you need to convey a sense of quality and professionalism, nothing beats custom embroidery. Using thread and needle, we can meticulously stitch your logo onto virtually any fabric. It's the perfect method for durable workwear, high-end polos, jackets, and of course, our signature custom hats and caps.

- **Explore Our Embroidery Content**
- **View Our Custom Embroidery Services**

### **DTG Printing: For Incredible Detail and Full-Color Images**

**Description:** Direct-to-Garment (DTG) printing is a revolutionary process that works like a high-tech inkjet printer for apparel. It's the ideal choice for designs with complex gradients, intricate details, or a wide range of colors. DTG is a fantastic option for small orders, prototypes, or any design that demands photo-realistic quality on cotton-based fabrics.

- **Learn More About DTG Printing**
- **View Our DTG Printing Services**

### **DTF Printing: The Versatile Solution for a Wide Range of Fabrics**

**Description:** Direct-to-Film (DTF) printing is the newest innovation in custom apparel. This versatile method prints a design onto a special film, which is then heat-pressed onto the garment. DTF is known for its ability to work on almost any fabric, including cotton, polyester, and blends. It produces vibrant, full-color designs with a soft feel, making it an excellent all-around choice for durability and flexibility.

## **Which Customization Method is Right for You? A Quick Comparison**

Still not sure which option is best? Use this handy chart to compare the key features of our most popular methods.

|  |  |  |  |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Method | Best For... | Pros | Cons |
| **Screen Printing** | Bulk orders, bold logos, simple designs, durability. | Extremely durable, cost-effective for large runs, vibrant colors. | Not ideal for complex photos or very small details, a higher minimum order quantity. |
| **Embroidery** | Professional uniforms, hats, jackets, high-end apparel. | Creates a premium, textured look; very durable; adds perceived value. | More expensive than printing; not suitable for very fine details or gradients. |
| **DTG Printing** | Small orders, full-color photos, complex designs, cotton fabrics. | No minimums, unlimited colors, soft feel, highly detailed results. | Best for cotton fabrics; less durable than screen printing for high-wear items. |
| **DTF Printing** | Mixed-fabric orders, complex designs, versatility. | Works on nearly all fabrics; bright colors; durable; soft feel; no minimums. | Newer technology, can have a slightly different feel than screen printing. |
