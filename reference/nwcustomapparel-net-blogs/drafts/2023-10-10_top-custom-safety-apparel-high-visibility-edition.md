## Breakdown of High Visibility Safety Apparel Standards

Where exactly do high visibility safety apparel standards come into play, and how can you ensure your gear meets them?

These standards are set by the American National Standards Institute (ANSI) and the International Safety Equipment Association (ISEA). They classify high visibility gear into three types based on the risk level of the work environment.

Type O is for off-road use, Type R is for roadway use, and Type P is for public safety. Each type has different requirements for the amount of reflective and fluorescent material.

You can ensure your gear meets these standards by checking the product label. It should state the ANSI/ISEA standard it meets. Also, the apparel should be appropriately sized to maintain visibility when worn over other clothing items.

## How to Choose the Right Custom Safety Apparel for Your Needs

While it's essential to prioritize safety, you must consider comfort and functionality when choosing the right custom safety apparel for your needs.

Begin by identifying the hazards in your workplace. Are you exposed to extreme heat, cold, or dangerous chemicals? You'll need gear designed to protect against those specific risks.

Also, consider the tasks you perform. Do you need a full range of motion or enhanced visibility? Choose apparel that supports your job's demands.

Finally, consider your comfort. It's no good having the safest gear if it's so uncomfortable you can't wear it. Look for breathable fabrics, adjustable features, and a good fit.
