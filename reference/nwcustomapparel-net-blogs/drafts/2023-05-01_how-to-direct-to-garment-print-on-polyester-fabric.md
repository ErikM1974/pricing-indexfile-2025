# How to direct to garment print on polyester fabric

I am going to try to help you better understand how to print on polyester fabric using Kornit direct to garment printers .  If you follow a recipe you will have excellent results, vibrant colors and customers that will keep on coming back for more T-shirts.
Have you ever wondered why printing on polyester fabric the color is muted and not as vibrant as 100% cotton?
 
Polyester fabric does not let the water-based inks soak into the fabric  like 100% cotton.  The inks and the pre-treat spread all over the T-shirt because of the polyester. The spreading of the pre-treat allows the ink to spider all over which is what we don’t want.  When the pretreat spreads all over the T-shirt and you go back to put your weight based down the ink will spread and spider and look horrible.
The trick to print on polyester is to lay down the pre-treat and wait at least 20 seconds for the pre-treat to settle down.  After 20 seconds print down the white base plate and wait another 20 seconds before you go back and print the color.
The dryer temperature is critical and the speed of the belt is very very important.  The temperature should be dropped down to around 270° and the belt speed should be 12 minutes .    This combination use in the Kornit direct to garment printers will produce excellent results on polyester fabric.
