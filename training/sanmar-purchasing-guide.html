Rule of thumb: an order isn't purchased until all three phases are done — costed, PO sent, and noted on the order. If you remember nothing else: cost it, send it, note it.

Why the API integration matters

ShopWorks talks directly to SanMar. Get Cost from Vendor pulls live cost and availability straight from SanMar — no manual price lookups — and Send PO transmits the purchase order electronically the moment you confirm it. No phone call, no email, no re-keying into sanmar.com.

Where this happens: everything in this guide is done inside ShopWorks OnSite, on the order you're purchasing for. Keep this page (or Bradley's guide) open next to ShopWorks on your first few orders.

2 · Phase 1 — Cost the Line Items

Step 1A — Open the line item and pull cost

  1. Open the line item. From the order, open the item in the Line Items window and stay on the Behavior tab.
  2. Check Product Source. Vendor should read 1002 · SANMAR with “Allow purchase” selected.
  3. Click “Get Cost from Vendor.” The blue button next to the vendor field opens the SanMar cost window.

Step 1B — Get Cost from SANMAR: color → cost → apply

The Get Cost from SANMAR window walks you through it, numbered 1–4:

  1. Select the color from the list. It must be a valid SanMar color for this exact product — e.g. Dark Hthr Grey. If your color isn't in the list, double-check the product number.
  2. Select the vendor. 1002 · SanMar.
  3. Press “Get Cost.” The live SanMar price comes back in the box.
  4. Press “Apply Cost.” Writes that cost onto the line item.
Watch out: the price isn't saved until you press Apply Cost — don't stop at Get Cost.

Step 1C — Every line item: one line per size, right column

  1. Repeat 1A–1B for all line items. Every line on the order gets costed the same way.
  2. Each size gets its own line item. e.g. K540, K540_2X, TLK540_XLT, TLK540_2XLT — never combined on one line.
  3. Special sizes carry an annotation. The suffix on the part number (_2X, _XLT…) — full cheat sheet in the next section.
  4. Quantities go in the 3X column. All special sizes use the 3X column — except 2X Mens garments, which use the 2X column.
Double-check here: wrong column = wrong size ordered. This is where mistakes usually happen — verify before building the PO.

4 · Phase 2 — Build & Send the PO

Step 2A — Create the PO on the Purchasing tab

  1. Open the Purchasing tab. Because you costed everything in Phase 1, your items appear under “Products to Purchase.”
  2. Check every line. Unit cost, vendor 1002 · Sanm, and quantities per size.
  3. Click “+ Add” under “Product POs for This Order” — this creates the SanMar PO and assigns it a PO number.
  4. Open the PO. Click the ✓ button at the far right of the new PO row to open it for editing.

Step 2B — Fill contact, ship-to and shipping method

  1. Vendor Contact. Use the ◄ Pick button to choose the SanMar contact (e.g. Dave Brown).
  2. Ship To. Use the green fill-in button to drop in the shop address — Northwest Embroidery, Milton WA.
  3. Ship Method. Set how it ships (e.g. PSST — SanMar's “Pack Separately, Ship Together” program that arrives via UPS). Account # and Net 30 terms fill in automatically.
Use the buttons, not the keyboard: each red circle in the screenshot marks a fill-in button — pick, don't type, so addresses and contacts come in exactly as SanMar expects.

Step 2C — Press “Send PO” and confirm

  1. Press “Send PO.” The button sits on the right-hand panel of the PO window.
  2. Confirm submission. A confirmation window appears — the PO isn't transmitted until you confirm it.
  3. Grab the PO number. Top-right of the window (e.g. 86738) — you'll want it for the order notes in Phase 3.
Done means sent: the PO goes to SanMar through the integration the moment you confirm — no separate email needed.

5 · Phase 3 — Close the Loop in the Order Notes

Thirty seconds that save everyone else

  1. Open “Notes On Order” — back on the order itself.
  2. Type at the front of the notes: your initials + date + “PO submitted” — the first thing anyone sees on the order.
  3. Flag out-of-stock items. Note any style on backorder and its estimated restock date.

Bradley's real example:

BW 5/17/21 PO submitted, ST650 on BO with est. restock of next week
initials + date status backorder + restock estimate
Why the front? Production and customer service read notes top-down — stock issues need to be the first thing they see.
