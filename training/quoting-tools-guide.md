# Which quoting tool do I use? — Sales rep guide

**The one rule first:** all three tools show the **same price**. They all pull live from the
same Caspio pricing tables, so a customer who sees a number in the online catalog, the rep
who quotes it over the phone, and the final order in ShopWorks all land on the **same dollar
amount**. You're never choosing between a "cheap" tool and an "expensive" tool — only between
*how much detail and follow-through* you need.

---

## The three tools at a glance

| Tool | Use it when… | What it gives you | Who drives it |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Customer Catalog** | A customer wants to browse and see ballpark pricing themselves | Basic pricing for customers | Customer (self-serve online) |
| **Quick Quote** | You need a fast price and don't need the full quote yet | Fast estimate — every method at once | Rep (no customer record) |
| **Full Quote Builder** | You're turning it into a real order | Comprehensive quote with shipping & size details — pushes to ShopWorks | Rep (full workup) |

---

## 1. Customer Catalog — "basic pricing for customers"
**When:** the customer is shopping around and wants to see prices themselves, online.
- It's the **customer-facing** site (`/catalog`). Customers reach it on their own.
- Shows a **simplified** price — it assumes a standard setup (e.g. an 8,000-stitch logo,
  bundled print locations) and quotes the standard size.
- Great for "give me a rough idea" — **not** for an exact, order-ready number.

## 2. Quick Quote — fast estimate (when you don't need the full quote)
**When:** you need a **fast price** and don't need the full workup yet — a customer on the
phone, a walk-in, or a quick "what would this run?" Phone is the most common case, but it's
for any fast quote, not only phone — including while you're entering something in ShopWorks.
- Type a **style + quantity + where the print goes**, see **every method priced at once**
  (Embroidery / DTG / Screen Print / DTF). No customer name, no shipping, no saving.
- More exact than the catalog: you can set the **real stitch count**, pick the **actual
  sizes** (incl. 2XL–6XL upcharges), choose **ink colors**, etc.
- Perfect for *"How much for 25 caps with a 12,000-stitch logo?"* or *"25 PC61, front + back?"*
- It's an **estimate tool** — it does **not** create a saved quote or push to ShopWorks.

## 3. Full Quote Builder — "comprehensive quote with shipping and size details"
**When:** the customer is ready to move forward and you're building the **real order**.
- These are the per-method builders on the dashboard: **Emb / Cap / Patch, DTG, Screen
  Print, DTF**.
- Captures the **customer**, the **full size breakdown**, **shipping**, fees, and notes;
  produces a **saved quote / PDF**; and **pushes to ShopWorks**.
- This is the tool of record. Use it for anything that becomes a quote you send or an order
  you process.

---

## Quick decision
- *Customer wants to look around themselves* → **Customer Catalog.**
- *Need a fast price, not the full workup yet* → **Quick Quote.**
- *We're writing this up / sending a quote / making it an order* → **Full Quote Builder.**

And again — whichever you reach for, **the price is the same**. If you ever see the three
disagree on the same garment, quantity, and decoration, tell Erik — that's a bug, not a
pricing difference.
